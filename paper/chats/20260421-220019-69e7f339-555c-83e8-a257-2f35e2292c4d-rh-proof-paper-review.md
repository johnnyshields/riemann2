So the honest theorem target is not “prove septic equality,” but rather “prove same reduced image germ at coincidence” or “prove collision-functoriality on the collision/cancellation locus.”  

The still-unwritten bridge is stronger: on good patches, any same-order local compensator should already be quotient-septic-visible. Equivalently, if the quotient-septic class vanishes, then there is no same-order local compensator. The strongest version is the conjectural one-dimensional first transverse quotient: after modding out odd reparametrization and lower scalar/value gauge, the first nontrivial local class should already be the \(r=q^{(7)}\)-line, and both the local section-corrected gap detector and the global quotient-septic detector should be nonzero on that same line, hence proportional. That would literally unify the local and global lanes. This is one of the strongest conceptual “goodies” of the whole attack, but it remains conjectural and I do not think it is in the paper.  

On the nonmixed/global affine side, the main theorem-level structural reduction is in the paper, but the sharpened frontier is not. The residual affine danger is no longer generic recurrence: it is an isolated same-parity sign-descent / middle-limited triple problem. In the later attack notes I sharpened the genuinely live affine obstruction to an isolated middle-limited triple satisfying
\[
c_j<r_j<c_{j-1},\qquad B_j(r_j)>0,
\]
subject to the triple-active-window constraint. That is the current best affine frontier beyond the already-in-draft endpoint-gap ceiling, and I do not think that exact middle-limited triple formulation is stably in the paper.  

The main negative findings, beyond what the draft already says, are these.

The naive source-summed corrected-block route is dead as a merger theorem route.  

No hidden finite-order mechanism can save the exact fixed-shear corner: no second transport variable, no third reset, no rescue from a two-pair model built only from separate descended one-atom states with natural merger law, and no explicit lobe-amplitude obstruction on the quartic–sextic rung.  

No second primitive explicit pointwise highest-new generator has emerged beyond \(r=q^{(7)}\), so if anything survives it is transport/global/package-level, not “one more local field.” 

Pure local scalar geometry is enough to realize the corrected overlap seed, so another purely local scalar normal-form argument will not close the overlap lane. That is one of the most useful later no-gos because it tells us exactly what type of theorem is **not** worth hunting.

And there are two important corrections / supersessions.

First, the full-chord \(\log \widehat Q'\) window-integral language I used at one point should be treated as obsolete, because the chord crosses the middle run where \(\widehat Q'\) changes sign and vanishes.

Second, the old transport story attached to \(B_1>B_0+B_2\) as “overlap” should be ignored; after the sign correction, the whole \(\Delta/\Xi\) lane belongs only to \(B_0+B_2>B_1\).

My current best view of the state of play is therefore this.

The close fixed-shear lane is now essentially a scalar theorem and should probably be written that way.

The overlap fixed-shear lane has reached a genuine local-scalar ceiling, so any further progress must come from shell-specific, global, or RH-specific structure.

The local good-patch lane is still best attacked through collision-cancellation functoriality and the projective edge law.

The honest order-7 lane is still best attacked through reduced-image germ or collision-functoriality, not raw septic equality.

The nonmixed/global affine lane has been reduced to RH-specific endpoint-gap or middle-limited triple exclusion, and not another round of pure pseudoline geometry.

That is the fullest honest recap of my attack and what I think it has added beyond the current paper.
