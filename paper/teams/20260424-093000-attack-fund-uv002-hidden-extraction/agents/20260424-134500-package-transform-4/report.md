## Claim

The strongest current Bottleneck D statement is transform-level and package-native, not the local proxy theorem on `\widetilde\Psi_{\min}`. In its sharpest present form:

after Bottleneck C canonically descends the actual corrected two-atom collision package to its corrected coincidence germ, the finite-core transformed scalars `\Xi_{\zeta,\le H}^{(N)}` should factor through that corrected coincidence package modulo directions that are `\Phi_K`-invisible through odd order `2N-1`; equivalently, on each corrected coincidence-package fiber, the minimal `N` with `\Xi_{\zeta,\le H}^{(N)}\neq 0` and its value are constant. The present draft only closes the quotient-level order-`7` package and still leaves raw representative ambiguity; nothing in the cited sections yet shows that the forgotten hidden directions are `\Phi_K`-invisible through the first surviving odd order. So the transform-level theorem is the right exact target, but it remains unproved.
