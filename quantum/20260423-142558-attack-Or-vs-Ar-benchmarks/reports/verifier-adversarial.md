# verifier-adversarial

- **Claim**: From the current concrete benchmark scope alone, the note should keep the split-role presentation, not move back toward privileging `O_r` alone. `O_r` remains the source-faithful value-channel-removed object, but the benchmark-level richer two-point outputs currently exhibited in the note are carried by the ambient `A_r = span{\psi}\oplus O_r` presentation. No benchmark here proves that `O_r` alone reproduces all of that two-point content, and no benchmark proves that `A_r` is the uniquely correct object in all roles.
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Proved: the note already records the exact relation `A_r = span{\psi} \oplus O_r`, with the explicit caution that the present record does not justify a unique global winner. Proved: the qutrit first-order benchmark’s richer-than-overlap datum is the singular-value/principal-angle output of the endpoint first-jet planes `A_1(t)=span{\psi(t),h_t}`; the explicit witness and formula are written at that level, not as an `O_1`-only theorem. Proved: the quartit second-order benchmark is likewise stated for ambient second osculating `3`-planes, again an `A_2`-type object, with singular values `(1,1,\sigma_3)` and an equal-overlap/different-`\sigma_3` witness pair. Proved: there is no cited benchmark computation here showing that the corresponding `O_r` principal-angle data alone already yields the same benchmark separation or fully subsumes the `A_r` benchmark output. Conditional: `O_r` is still the better match to the value-channel-removed motivation, and from benchmark scope alone nothing rules out a future reformulation where `O_r` becomes the preferred two-point object. Missing: an explicit benchmark-level `O_r` calculation on the qutrit and quartit families, compared side-by-side with `A_r`, proving either equivalence or strict inferiority/superiority. Honest verdict: these benchmarks support keeping both roles visible, with `O_r` as the faithful horizontal object and `A_r` as the object on which the current strongest transport-free two-point benchmark statements are actually proved.
