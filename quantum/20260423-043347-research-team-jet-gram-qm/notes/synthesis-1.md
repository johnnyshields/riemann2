# Synthesis 1

The first research-team cycle narrowed the honest claim.

## Surviving core

- The paper-side object is best described, for this track, as a **whitened
  local jet cross-Gram block**.
- There is a plausible first-order bridge to standard quantum geometry through
  horizontal derivatives of pure-state families.
- Toy models already show that the algebraic pattern can appear on the quantum
  side.

## Main failure mode

- The naive higher-jet matrix analogue leaks coordinates.
- Higher projected coordinate jets mix under phase gauge and reparameterization.
- Bilateral whitening is not invariant under the resulting non-orthogonal jet
  coordinate changes.

## Consequence

- The strongest honest invariant may be weaker than a canonical matrix. It may
  live on transported jet subspaces via singular values or principal angles.

## Next targets

1. Pin down the first-order reduction to Fubini-Study / QGT cleanly.
2. Build at least one nonconstant example with asymmetric same-point blocks.
3. Decide whether the meaningful object is matrix-valued or only subspace-valued.
