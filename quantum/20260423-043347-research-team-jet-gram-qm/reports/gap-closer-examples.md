# gap-closer-examples

- **Claim**: The two best toy examples are: (1) a one-parameter qubit great-circle family, for example \(|\psi(\phi)\rangle=\cos(\phi/2)|0\rangle+\sin(\phi/2)|1\rangle\), which is the cleanest first worked example because it gives an exact \(2\times2\) same-point jet Gram block, an exact mixed block, and after whitening the cross block becomes a literal rotation matrix \(\Omega(\Delta)=\begin{pmatrix}\cos(\Delta/2)&\sin(\Delta/2)\\-\sin(\Delta/2)&\cos(\Delta/2)\end{pmatrix}\); (2) a one-parameter real coherent-state displacement family, where the same-point block is constant and the mixed block is exact, yielding a Gaussian-Hermite transport block after whitening. The first worked example in the quantum track should be the qubit great-circle family.
- **Status**: heuristic
- **Evidence**: Proved: the paper’s source object is a whitened cross block built from two same-point jet Gram blocks and one mixed jet block. The qubit great-circle family is an exact finite-dimensional testbed: \(\langle\psi|\partial_\phi\psi\rangle=0\), the same-point block is \(M=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&1/4\end{pmatrix}\), the mixed block is \(N(\Delta)=\begin{pmatrix}\cos(\Delta/2)&\frac12\sin(\Delta/2)\\-\frac12\sin(\Delta/2)&\frac14\cos(\Delta/2)\end{pmatrix}\), and whitening yields the rotation matrix above. The coherent-state displacement family is also exact: with standard real coherent-state parametrization, \(\langle\alpha|\partial_\alpha\alpha\rangle=0\) and the same-point block is \(I_2\), so whitening is trivial and the mixed block itself shows Gaussian-Hermite structure. Conditional: these examples fit the paper’s pattern provided the quantum-side jet vectors are defined as the state and its orthogonal projected derivative along a one-parameter family, or equivalently a parallel-transport gauge representative. The qubit example suggests that same-point blocks encode local metric scale while the whitened mixed block captures dimensionless transport between nearby fibers. The coherent-state example suggests the same formalism can interpolate from local geometry to overlap decay. Missing: a source-grounded abstract definition that is fully gauge-invariant and not just a convenient one-parameter choice; a precise statement of how the first jet level reduces to standard QGT/Fubini-Study data; a compelling scattering example.
- **Exact refs**: `paper/proof_of_rh.tex:114`; `paper/proof_of_rh.tex:171-215`; `paper/proof_of_rh.tex:226-259`; `quantum/findings.md:19-38`; `quantum/20260423-043347-research-team-jet-gram-qm/notes/coordinator-brief.md:21-25,34-39`.
- **Dependencies**: Adopt a one-parameter normalized state family \(|\psi(\lambda)\rangle\). Define the jet pair by \(|\psi(\lambda)\rangle\) and the orthogonal projected derivative \((I-|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)\partial_\lambda|\psi\rangle\), or an equivalent parallel-transport gauge. Accept that these toy models are analogues of the paper’s jet-Gram structure, not derivations of the zeta-side formulas. For the coherent example, use the standard coherent-state overlap formula.
- **Strongest objection**: The qubit and coherent-state families are almost too clean: their same-point blocks are constant, so whitening is easy and may hide the main difficulty. The paper’s object has asymmetric left/right blocks, explicit local-derivative dependence, and a nontrivial finite-separation normalization. These toy models show the shape of the construction, but not yet the full difficulty of a genuinely curved or inhomogeneous quantum family.
- **Needed for promotion**: Write the abstract quantum-side definition of same-point jet Gram blocks, mixed blocks, and whitening in a gauge-invariant way. Include the qubit great-circle example as the first worked example, with the exact matrices above. Include the coherent-state displacement example as the second worked example. Only after those are fixed, test whether a squeezed-state or scattering family adds genuinely new structure.
