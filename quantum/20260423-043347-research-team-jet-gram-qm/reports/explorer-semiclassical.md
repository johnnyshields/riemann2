# explorer-semiclassical

- **Claim**: The whitened mixed block has a serious but limited semiclassical analogue: it is best read as a normalized local channel-coupling or transport diagnostic between two nearby jet frames, not as a full scattering matrix. In the paper’s formulas, the same-point blocks \(G_{m,\pm}(s)\) are local jet metrics and the mixed block \(N_m(s)\) is their cross-jet coupling; whitening in \(\widehat\Omega_\zeta(s;m)=G_{m,-}(s)^{-1/2}N_m(s)G_{m,+}(s)^{-1/2}\) removes local scale and basis conditioning, exactly as one would do to compare two local wave frames or two channel subspaces. The honest semiclassical reading is therefore: “cross-Gram of orthonormalized local amplitude/slope frames.” That is close to transport language and can shadow a transmission block in simple 1D settings, but no true scattering structure, unitarity, flux conservation, or composition law follows from the source formulas alone. The relation to effective Hamiltonians is weaker still: at most a formal analogy with a metric-normalized off-diagonal block between two locally chosen subspaces.
- **Status**: heuristic
- **Evidence**: Proved: the source object is explicitly a whitened mixed block built from two same-point jet Gram matrices and one cross block in both jet-limit and finite-separation forms. The coordinates are literally local value/derivative coordinates obtained by the point-to-jet map \(P_h\). Because whitening uses the positive same-point blocks on the left and right, the resulting matrix is naturally interpreted as the mixed block after local normalization of each endpoint frame. Conditional: in semiclassical or wave language, the clean comparison is not to a global \(S\)-matrix but to a normalized transmission/coupling block between two nearby local frames. In 1D scattering, if one chooses at \(x_\pm=m\pm s/2\) a two-component local state \((\psi,\psi')\) or its WKB/coherent-state analogue, then a whitened cross-covariance between the two sides is exactly the same algebraic type as \(\widehat\Omega_\zeta\). Jet data across nearby points has a clean wavepacket interpretation if one works in a Gaussian/coherent-state family or local WKB frame. Best first tests: 1D Schrödinger barrier scattering at fixed energy; quadratic Hamiltonians / Gaussian wavepackets; Helmholtz or random-wave local diagnostics. Missing: a state-family construction showing that the quantum/semiclassical jet vectors are gauge-invariant and not presentation-dependent; a derived propagation law or composition rule for the whitened block; any proof that the block is flux-unitary, symplectic, contractive, or otherwise constrained like true scattering/transfer objects; a derivation from an actual effective Hamiltonian or Feshbach/Schur complement construction rather than a similarity in matrix shape.

- **Dependencies**: Positivity/invertibility of the same-point jet Gram blocks so whitening is defined. A concrete choice of local wave/state family: raw field jets, coherent states, WKB branches, Jost data, or another local frame. A gauge rule for phase/frame changes, especially if one wants a quantum-state rather than classical-wave interpretation. For any stronger transport claim, an explicit evolution/scattering law relating the two endpoints.
- **Strongest objection**: True scattering or transfer objects come from an evolution equation, boundary matching, or conserved flux and therefore satisfy composition or normalization constraints. The paper’s \(\widehat\Omega_\zeta\) is only a normalized mixed Gram block. From this source scope alone, it need not compose along intermediate points, need not be unitary or symplectic, and need not encode propagation rather than mere correlation.
- **Needed for promotion**: Build one explicit 1D test where \((\psi,\psi')\) or a coherent-state jet is used to form same-point and mixed Gram blocks, then check whether the whitened block tracks known transfer/scattering data. Prove or disprove a short-distance composition law for three nearby points. Give a gauge-invariant state-family definition of the jet vectors and show how the first nontrivial level reduces to a standard local geometric quantity. If the effective-Hamiltonian analogy is to be kept, derive the whitened block from a concrete projected resolvent / Feshbach / DtN setup.
