# gap-degenerate-nogo

- **Claim**: Let \(S_-,S_+\) be finite-dimensional subspaces of a common Hilbert space, let \(C:=\Pi_-|_{S_+}:S_+\to S_-\) be the canonical cross-contraction, and let \(C=V|C|\) be its canonical polar decomposition. Fix a nonzero principal value \(\sigma\in(0,1]\) of multiplicity \(m\ge 2\), and set
  \[
  E_+(\sigma):=\ker(|C|-\sigma I)\subset S_+,
  \qquad
  E_-(\sigma):=V E_+(\sigma)\subset S_-.
  \]
  Then the pair \((S_-,S_+)\) canonically determines the degenerate principal subspaces \(E_\pm(\sigma)\) and the block isometry \(V_\sigma:=V|_{E_+(\sigma)}:E_+(\sigma)\to E_-(\sigma)\), with \(C|_{E_+(\sigma)}=\sigma V_\sigma\). But there is no canonical orthonormal principal frame on this block from the subspace pair alone: more precisely, no assignment of an ordered orthonormal basis of \(E_+(\sigma)\) and the matched basis of \(E_-(\sigma)\) can be defined from \((S_-,S_+)\) in a unitary-equivariant way. Repeated nonzero principal values leave an unavoidable internal \(U(m)\) freedom, so the strongest exact canonical object on the degenerate nonzero sector is the basis-free partial isometry \(V_\sigma\), not a frame or matrix representative.
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Proved: the current record already gives the canonical operator \(C\), its polar factor \(V\), and the fact that simple nonzero spectrum is exactly the hypothesis under which canonical principal vectors exist. On a repeated block \(E_+(\sigma)\), one has \(|C|=\sigma I\), hence \(C|_{E_+(\sigma)}=\sigma V_\sigma\). Therefore every orthonormal basis \((e_i)\) of \(E_+(\sigma)\) yields a valid matched principal frame \((V_\sigma e_i,e_i)\), and replacing \((e_i)\) by \((Ue_i)\) for any \(U\in U(m)\) gives another equally valid principal frame with the same subspace-pair data. The obstruction is exact: in the model block where \(E_+=E_-=\mathbb C^m\) and \(C|_{E_+}=\sigma I\), the whole group \(U(m)\) preserves the subspace pair and the operator \(C\), but no ordered orthonormal basis is fixed by all of \(U(m)\) when \(m\ge2\). So any putative canonical frame rule depending only on the subspace pair and natural under unitary symmetries is impossible. Conditional: if one adds extra symmetry-breaking structure that splits the repeated block, a frame can be chosen. Missing: nothing stronger than the basis-free block operator \(V_\sigma\) is canonically forced by the subspace pair alone on the degenerate sector.

- **Dependencies**: Finite-dimensional principal-angle theory for a pair of subspaces; the canonical cross-contraction \(C=\Pi_-|_{S_+}\); its canonical polar decomposition \(C=V|C|\); the identification of repeated nonzero principal values with repeated nonzero singular values of \(C\); the naturality requirement that any “canonical from the pair alone” frame choice must be invariant/equivariant under ambient unitaries preserving the subspace pair.
- **Strongest objection**: The no-go theorem needs the symmetry/naturality reading of “canonical.” Without requiring equivariance under automorphisms of the subspace pair, one can always choose a basis by arbitrary external conventions, but that is exactly extra frame-selecting structure rather than something determined by the pair itself.
- **Needed for promotion**: State the result explicitly as a unitary-equivariant no-go theorem for repeated nonzero principal blocks, and present it as the sharp negative complement to the positive polar-transport theorem: simple spectrum gives canonical vectors; repeated nonzero spectrum leaves only canonical subspaces plus the block partial isometry \(V_\sigma\).
