# explorer-qgt

- **Claim**: The best honest quantum-side formulation is: for a smooth family of normalized pure states \(\lambda \mapsto |\psi(\lambda)\rangle\), choose the horizontal derivatives \(D_a|\psi\rangle := (1-|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)\partial_a|\psi\rangle\), extend to higher projected jets \(D_{a_1}\cdots D_{a_k}|\psi\rangle\), form same-point jet Gram blocks and a mixed cross block between nearby parameter values, then whiten the mixed block by the same-point blocks. In this formulation, the lowest horizontal \(1\)-jet block reproduces the standard pure-state quantum geometric tensor data (\(\Re Q\) = Fubini-Study metric, \(2\Im Q\) = Berry curvature, \(4\Re Q\) = pure-state quantum Fisher information), while the full whitened mixed jet block is best described as a higher-jet finite-separation extension of that first-order geometry, not as a standard foundational QM tensor.
- **Status**: heuristic
- **Evidence**: Proved: the paper’s object is structurally a whitened local cross-Gram block built from two same-point Gram matrices and one mixed block, with whitening \(M_1^{-1/2}N_{12}M_2^{-1/2}\) in the jet limit and \(G_{m,-}(s)^{-1/2}N_m(s)G_{m,+}(s)^{-1/2}\) at finite separation; this is explicit in the source formulas. Proved: in standard pure-state quantum geometry, the gauge-invariant first-order object is the horizontal derivative Gram matrix \(Q_{ab}=\langle D_a\psi|D_b\psi\rangle\); its real part gives the Fubini-Study metric, its imaginary part gives Berry curvature up to the standard factor, and pure-state QFI is \(4\Re Q\). Therefore a projected same-point first-jet Gram construction does naturally reproduce known first-order quantum geometry. Conditional: to match the paper’s value/derivative \(2\times 2\) block, one must project away the value channel on the quantum side, because the physically intrinsic geometry lives on rays rather than raw state vectors. After that projection, the analogue of the paper’s “whitened mixed block” is a normalized finite-separation overlap/transport matrix between horizontal jet frames at two nearby parameter values. This is plausibly interpretable as finite-separation transport plus higher-order nonadiabatic shape data, or as canonical correlations/principal-angle data between local horizontal-jet subspaces. Conditional: the mixed jet-whitened block is stronger than the infinitesimal QGT because it compares two nearby local frames rather than only one tangent space; it is weaker than a canonical quantum connection because, from this scope alone, no unique operational transport rule or observable is fixed. Missing: a canonical gauge-invariant definition of higher projected jets beyond first order, especially in multiparameter families where covariant differentiation and symmetrization choices matter. Missing: an operational interpretation of the full whitened mixed block analogous to fidelity susceptibility, adiabatic response, interferometric phase, or a metrological quantity. Missing: a worked model showing whether the higher-jet block captures genuinely new information beyond derivatives of QGT/Berry/FS/QFI. Literature anchors / standard concepts: Provost-Vallee’s quantum geometric tensor, Berry’s adiabatic phase/curvature, Fubini-Study metric on projective Hilbert space, and Braunstein-Caves/Helstrom quantum Fisher information.

External anchors: Provost and Vallee (1980); Berry (1989); Braunstein and Caves (1994); Helstrom; standard Fubini-Study projective metric.
- **Dependencies**: Standard pure-state quantum geometry for smooth ray families; existence of normalized state families and horizontal projection \(1-|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|\); positive-definite same-point horizontal jet Gram blocks so whitening is well-defined; a multiparameter extension if one wants the full tensorial QGT rather than only a one-parameter scalar reduction.
- **Strongest objection**: The paper’s local object is a whitened overlap matrix of chosen value/derivative jets at two distinct points, whereas the standard QGT/Berry/Fubini-Study/QFI data are intrinsic first-order tensors on projective Hilbert space at a single point. From this scope alone, the higher-jet mixed block may be only a normalized correlation/transport matrix for a chosen horizontal-jet frame, not a canonical quantum-geometric structure. In particular, higher jets require nonunique projection/covariantization choices, and the whitening step has no standard quantum-metrological meaning by itself.
- **Needed for promotion**: Write a clean abstract state-family definition of the projected-jet construction; prove gauge invariance under \(|\psi(\lambda)\rangle \mapsto e^{i\theta(\lambda)}|\psi(\lambda)\rangle\) and reparameterization covariance; show explicitly that the first horizontal block reduces to \(Q_{ab}\), \(g_{ab}=\Re Q_{ab}\), \(F_{ab}=2\Im Q_{ab}\), and \(I_{ab}=4\Re Q_{ab}\); compute one toy example to see what the finite-separation whitened mixed block contains beyond QGT; determine whether that extra data is merely the finite-separation transport of QGT derivatives or a genuinely new invariant.
