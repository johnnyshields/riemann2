# verifier-adversarial

- **Claim**: The current quantum-side track has not yet identified a meaningful quantum-geometric object in the same sense as the paper’s normalized local jet-Gramian. What it has identified, at best, is a candidate family of transported overlap invariants for horizontal jet subspaces. Before any stronger claim, the track must weaken the object from a canonical matrix-valued “quantum geometry” to a **whitened local jet cross-Gram analogue**, rename it accordingly, and repair three failures: parameter dependence, higher-jet gauge/coordinate leakage, and the absence of a canonical mixed two-point transport rule.
- **Status**: rejected
- **Evidence**: Proved: the cited paper defines a very specific matrix object: a local value/derivative jet normalization via \(P_h\), same-point jet blocks \(J(T)\) and \(G_{m,\pm}(s)\), a mixed block \(N_{12}\) or \(N_m(s)\), and bilateral whitening to \(\Omega_\infty(T_1,T_2)\) and \(\widehat\Omega_\zeta(s;m)\). This is a concrete whitened cross-Gram construction in a chosen jet coordinate system, not a projective-Hilbert-space tensor. Proved: the strongest deposited report on invariance says the naive higher-jet quantum matrix is not gauge-invariant or reparameterization-covariant; higher projected coordinate jets mix lower jets, and whitening is not invariant under the resulting non-orthogonal triangular changes. Its repaired invariant is narrower: orthonormal jet subspaces plus their singular values/principal angles after parallel transport. Proved: this directly undercuts the stronger state-family report, which claims gauge invariance of higher transported jets and presents a matrix \(\Omega_m(\lambda_1,\lambda_2)\) as a clean analogue without resolving the higher-jet coordinate-mixing problem. Proved: the examples report uses the raw state vector together with a projected derivative and obtains an exact rotation matrix in a qubit family, but that construction is deliberately one-parameter, constant-metric, and hides the main obstruction; it does not test the asymmetric left/right whitening, higher-jet mixing, or nontrivial transport dependence that the paper’s formulas actually have. Conditional: the first horizontal jet does line up with standard one-point quantum geometry, and a transported, orthonormalized jet-subspace comparison may be a legitimate finite-separation invariant; that is the honest surviving core of the analogy. Missing: a canonical higher-jet covariant derivative convention; a proof that the relevant parameter is intrinsic rather than chosen; a canonical two-point transport prescription; a proof that the matrix entries themselves, rather than only singular values/principal angles, are invariant; and an operational interpretation beyond normalized correlation/overlap. Honest verdict: from this scope alone, the formulation breaks at the point where it tries to promote a basis-dependent whitened mixed jet matrix into a canonical quantum-geometric object.

- **Dependencies**: A normalized state-family model on ray space; an explicit higher-jet covariantization rule; Berry/Pancharatnam or another canonical transport between endpoints; a decision on whether the invariant lives on matrices, unitary orbits, or only singular values/principal angles; an intrinsic control parameter if reparameterization-independence is claimed; invertible same-point jet Gram blocks on the intended jet span.
- **Strongest objection**: The paper’s object is an explicit whitened matrix in value/derivative jet coordinates, while the quantum-side object becomes canonical only after quotienting out exactly the coordinate information that fixes such a matrix. Once gauge and reparameterization issues are handled honestly, the natural invariant is no longer the full matrix but the relative position of transported jet subspaces.
- **Needed for promotion**: Rename the current target to **whitened local jet cross-Gram analogue** or **transported horizontal-jet overlap invariant**. Weaken all matrix-valued claims to singular values/principal angles unless an explicit canonical frame theorem is proved. Repair the state-family proposal so it agrees with the gauge report on higher-jet mixing. Prove the first nontrivial reduction to standard quantum geometry cleanly. Give one nontrivial worked family with nonconstant same-point blocks and asymmetric endpoint whitening.
