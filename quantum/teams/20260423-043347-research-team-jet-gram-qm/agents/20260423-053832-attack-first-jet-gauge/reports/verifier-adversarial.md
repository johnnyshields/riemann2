# verifier-adversarial

- **Claim**: The note should not claim an unconditional gauge upgrade of the one-parameter first-horizontal-jet two-point matrix to a canonical ray-space invariant. The strongest safe statement is narrower: for a one-parameter \(C^1\) pure-state family, the first horizontal jet \(h_\lambda=(I-|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)\partial_\lambda\psi\) is gauge-covariant and its same-point Gram norm is gauge invariant; and if one additionally fixes a two-point transport rule \(U_{1\leftarrow 2}\) that is itself defined on ray space and transforms gauge-covariantly, then the resulting transported first-jet mixed block, and hence its bilaterally whitened \(2\times2\) block, is invariant under change of local lift. What is not justified from the present scope alone is that this produces a canonical matrix-valued invariant independent of the transport convention.
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Proved: the same-point horizontal Gram data is gauge invariant and is the clean one-point bridge to QGT/Fubini-Study/Berry data. Proved: for one parameter, the first projected jet transforms covariantly under phase gauge, so its norm is gauge invariant. Proved: mixed two-point overlaps are not gauge invariant unless transport is inserted. Conditional: with a fixed gauge-covariant transport rule such as Berry/Pancharatnam transport, the transported mixed block can be made invariant under lift change. Missing: any deposited proof that the chosen transport convention is canonical, globally available, convention-independent, or free of endpoint-overlap/path ambiguities; therefore the upgrade from “gauge-covariant transported-frame construction” to “canonical gauge-invariant two-point matrix on ray space” is still blocked.

- **Dependencies**: One-dimensional \(C^1\) normalized pure-state family; first horizontal jet \(h_\lambda=P\partial_\lambda\psi\); nonvanishing endpoint horizontal norms so whitening is defined; a transport rule \(U_{1\leftarrow 2}\) specified on ray space with explicit gauge-covariance property; fixed endpoint ordering; first-order only.
- **Strongest objection**: The apparent gauge upgrade may be only covariance disguised as invariance. If \(U_{1\leftarrow 2}\) is merely chosen after choosing lifts, or if different legitimate Berry/Pancharatnam conventions give different matrices, then the matrix is not a canonical ray-space object. Endpoint phases are harmless only once the transport law is specified to absorb them.
- **Needed for promotion**: State the gauge claim in the note only in this form: after fixing a gauge-covariant two-point transport convention on ray space, the one-parameter first-horizontal-jet transported mixed block and its bilaterally whitened block are independent of the chosen local lift. Do not call it canonical. To promote further, prove transport-independence or reduce to subspace data.
