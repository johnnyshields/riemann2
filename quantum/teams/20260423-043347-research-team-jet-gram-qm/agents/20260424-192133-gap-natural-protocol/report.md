# UV-014 natural protocol report

## Claim

For finite-rank endpoint subspaces \(S_\pm\) with orthogonal projectors
\(\Pi_\pm\), coherent reflection access gives a natural dynamical protocol for
the principal-angle/operator package. Define
\[
R_-:=2\Pi_- - I,\qquad R_+:=2\Pi_+ - I,\qquad W:=R_-R_+ .
\]
On each nontrivial Jordan principal two-plane for the pair \((S_-,S_+)\), the
unitary \(W\) has eigenphases \(\pm 2\theta_i\), where \(\theta_i\) is the
corresponding principal angle. Thus phase estimation on \(W\), or equivalently
the alternating-reflection/amplitude-amplification dynamics generated by
\(R_-R_+\), estimates the same principal angles that the operator package
records.

This is a positive UV-014 closure under explicit access hypotheses: it is more
operational than the single sequential yes/yes projector test because it gives a
coherent repeated-reflection dynamics whose observable phases are the principal
angles themselves. It is not stronger invariant content than the existing
subspace/operator package.

## Evidence

Proved. In the standard two-subspace Jordan decomposition, outside the common
intersection, orthogonal residual, and unequal-dimension leftover sectors, each
principal pair spans a two-plane with orthonormal basis \(e_i,f_i\) such that
\(e_i\in S_-\) and
\[
S_+\cap \operatorname{span}\{e_i,f_i\}
  =\operatorname{span}\{\cos\theta_i\,e_i+\sin\theta_i\,f_i\}.
\]
In this plane,
\[
R_-=\begin{pmatrix}1&0\\0&-1\end{pmatrix},
\qquad
R_+=
\begin{pmatrix}
\cos 2\theta_i & \sin 2\theta_i\\
\sin 2\theta_i & -\cos 2\theta_i
\end{pmatrix},
\]
so
\[
R_-R_+=
\begin{pmatrix}
\cos 2\theta_i & \sin 2\theta_i\\
-\sin 2\theta_i & \cos 2\theta_i
\end{pmatrix}
\]
has eigenvalues \(e^{\pm 2i\theta_i}\), up to the sign convention for rotation
orientation. The invariant information is therefore the same multiset of
principal angles already encoded by the singular values of the cross-contraction
and by the spectrum of \(\Pi_-\Pi_+\Pi_-|_{S_-}\).

Conditional on implementation. The protocol needs coherent access to the
reflections \(R_\pm\), not only destructive projective measurements. This
hypothesis is natural in settings where the endpoint subspaces are code spaces,
symmetry sectors, eigenspaces with phase-kickback access, or any experimentally
implemented yes/no subspace test that can be coherently converted into a sign
flip. With phase estimation on controlled \(W=R_-R_+\), an input with support in
the relevant Jordan sectors returns phases \(\pm2\theta_i\) with weights set by
the input overlaps. To recover the full multiset with multiplicities one needs
state preparation or sampling that covers the endpoint subspace, for example
principal-vector preparations, a spanning ensemble, or a maximally mixed state
over \(S_-\). Degenerate principal angles are observable as degenerate phases,
but the protocol does not select canonical vectors inside a degenerate block.

Missing. This does not provide a new physical Hamiltonian for arbitrary jet
families, and it does not turn the whitened matrix representative into a
canonical matrix. It also does not remove the known Kato path-dependence or the
curvature obstruction to endpoint-independent transport. The output is exactly
the endpoint principal-angle spectrum, accessed through a coherent experimental
primitive.

No scripts were needed; the route is the standard algebraic two-reflection
calculation on each principal two-plane.

## Baseline / delta

Baseline: the prior operational report proves only the thin yes/yes reading:
\(\Pi_-\Pi_+\Pi_-|_{S_-}\) is the effect operator for a sequential subspace
membership test, with eigenvalues \(\cos^2\theta_i\). The exact unitary/Krylov,
compressed-propagator, commutator/leakage, short-time, and Kato reports give
dynamical reformulations, but either specialize to a chosen unitary orbit or
realize a whole projector path rather than give a direct endpoint protocol.

Delta: the two-reflection protocol upgrades the operational reading from a
single projective probability test to a coherent endpoint experiment. The
measurable phase spectrum of \(R_-R_+\) is \(\{\pm2\theta_i\}\), so the protocol
estimates the principal angles directly rather than only the yes/yes
probabilities. The gain is operational and dynamical; the invariant content
remains the same principal-angle/operator package.

## Dependencies

Finite-rank closed endpoint subspaces, orthogonal projectors \(\Pi_\pm\), and
coherent implementation of \(R_\pm=2\Pi_\pm-I\). The full-spectrum protocol also
needs enough state preparation or sampling over \(S_-\) to see all Jordan
sectors. The mathematical phase claim depends only on the standard principal
angle/Jordan decomposition for two subspaces.

## Strongest objection

The protocol is a repackaging at the invariant level: it uses the same endpoint
projectors and recovers the same principal angles already present in
\(\Pi_-\Pi_+\Pi_-|_{S_-}\), \(C\), \(K_\pm\), and the polar/operator package. Its
claim to UV-014 closure is not new mathematical content, but a more natural
coherent measurement and alternating-reflection dynamics under reflection-access
hypotheses. If the projectors are not coherently reflectable in the intended
physical setting, the route collapses back to the earlier thin yes/yes test.
