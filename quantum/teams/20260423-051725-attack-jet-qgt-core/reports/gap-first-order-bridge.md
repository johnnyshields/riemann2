# gap-first-order-bridge

- **Claim**: First-order bridge, stated honestly: let \(\lambda=(\lambda^a)\mapsto |\psi(\lambda)\rangle\) be a smooth normalized pure-state family and set \(P(\lambda):=I-|\psi(\lambda)\rangle\langle\psi(\lambda)|\), \(|D_a\psi\rangle:=P\,\partial_a|\psi\rangle\). Then the quantum object that exactly matches the paper’s “remove the value channel, then take a same-point jet Gram block” pattern is the first horizontal-jet Gram matrix
  \[
  Q_{ab}(\lambda):=\langle D_a\psi|D_b\psi\rangle
  =\langle \partial_a\psi|(I-|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)|\partial_b\psi\rangle,
  \]
  whose real part is the Fubini-Study metric \(g_{ab}=\Re Q_{ab}\), whose imaginary part gives Berry curvature \(F_{ab}=2\,\Im Q_{ab}\), and whose symmetric metrological tensor is the pure-state quantum Fisher information \(I_{ab}=4\,\Re Q_{ab}\). This is the exact first-order reduction. What does not reduce to standard quantum geometry is the paper’s full two-point whitened matrix pattern; on the quantum side that can only be represented, after inserting a transport rule between fibers, as a normalized two-point overlap/transport matrix between horizontal tangent frames, and it is not itself standard Fubini-Study/QGT/Berry data nor a canonical higher-jet invariant from the present scope alone.
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Proved: the paper’s local object is explicitly a same-point Gram / mixed cross-block / bilateral-whitening construction, first in the jet-limit form \(\Omega_\infty(T_1,T_2)=M_1^{-1/2}N_{12}M_2^{-1/2}\) and then at finite separation as \(\widehat\Omega_\zeta(s;m)=G_{m,-}(s)^{-1/2}N_m(s)G_{m,+}(s)^{-1/2}\). Proved: the paper also states that this construction removes the dominant value channel and keeps the intrinsic local deformation. Proved: on the quantum side, the exact first-order analogue of “remove the value channel” is orthogonal projection off the ray direction by \(P=I-|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|\). The resulting same-point first horizontal-jet Gram matrix is exactly \(Q_{ab}=\langle D_a\psi|D_b\psi\rangle\), which is the standard pure-state quantum geometric tensor; hence \(g_{ab}=\Re Q_{ab}\), \(F_{ab}=2\Im Q_{ab}\), and \(I_{ab}=4\Re Q_{ab}\). Proved: a raw two-point mixed overlap \(\langle D_a\psi(\lambda_1)|D_b\psi(\lambda_2)\rangle\) is not gauge invariant without a transport convention, and higher projected coordinate jets mix under phase gauge and reparameterization; therefore a bilaterally whitened higher-jet matrix is not canonically invariant as a matrix object. Conditional: after Berry parallel transport \(U_{1\leftarrow2}\), one may define a first-order transported mixed block and whiten it by the endpoint same-point blocks, but this is a finite-separation transport/correlation matrix between tangent frames, not standard one-point QGT/Berry/Fubini-Study data. Missing: a canonical higher-jet covariantization and a proof that the full whitened matrix, rather than only transported jet-subspace data such as singular values or principal angles, is intrinsic.

- **Dependencies**: Standard pure-state projective Hilbert-space geometry for smooth normalized state families; the horizontal projector \(P=I-|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|\); the standard identity for the QGT; Berry parallel transport if one wants any gauge-invariant two-point comparison; positive-definiteness of the first horizontal Gram block on the tangent directions retained so that whitening is defined.
- **Strongest objection**: The paper’s matrix is a concrete value/derivative two-point whitened block with explicit entries, while the exact quantum first-order identification lands only on the same-point horizontal tangent Gram \(Q_{ab}\). The moment one asks for a two-point whitened matrix analogue, one must choose transport and parameter/frame conventions; under general reparameterizations and higher-jet gauge mixing, matrix entries are not canonical.
