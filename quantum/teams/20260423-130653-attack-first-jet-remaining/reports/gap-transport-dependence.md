# gap-transport-dependence

- **Claim**: For the one-parameter first-horizontal-jet construction, once a gauge-covariant transport rule \(U_{-\leftarrow +}\) is fixed as part of the data, the bilaterally whitened block has no remaining local-lift ambiguity; it is exactly well defined for that chosen rule. The unresolved ambiguity is therefore transport-rule dependence. From the present hypotheses alone, that dependence is larger than a single global phase on the whole whitened matrix: after normalizing \(e_\pm:=h_{\lambda_\pm}/\|h_{\lambda_\pm}\|\),
  \[
  \Omega(U)=
  \begin{pmatrix}
  \langle \psi_-|U\psi_+\rangle & \langle \psi_-|Ue_+\rangle\\
  \langle e_-|U\psi_+\rangle & \langle e_-|Ue_+\rangle
  \end{pmatrix},
  \]
  so changing \(U\) changes these transported overlaps directly. Hence the smallest correct statement is: the theorem gives a ray-curve-with-chosen-transport invariant, not a transport-independent endpoint matrix invariant; absent stronger transport axioms, the remaining ambiguity is genuinely matrix-entry dependence, at least in the derivative-channel column/row, not merely a global phase.
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Proved: the cited gauge result already establishes that, for fixed gauge-covariant \(U_{-\leftarrow +}\), \(\Omega(U)\) is independent of lift choice, and the reparameterization result establishes exact invariance under orientation-preserving one-parameter changes. Proved: because the same-point blocks are \(M_\pm=\mathrm{diag}(1,\|h_{\lambda_\pm}\|^2)\), whitening only replaces \(h_\pm\) by the normalized vectors \(e_\pm\), giving the displayed formula. Proved: this formula shows that all remaining freedom sits in the action of the chosen transport on the two-dimensional source span \(\mathrm{span}\{\psi_+,e_+\}\). There is no theorem in the cited materials forcing two admissible gauge-covariant rules \(U,V\) to satisfy \(\Omega(V)=e^{i\theta}\Omega(U)\). Proved by direct linear-algebra counterexample: one can choose \(U\) and \(V\) so that \(\Omega(U)=I\) while \(\Omega(V)\) has a nonzero upper-right entry, hence is not a global-phase multiple. Conditional: if one adds stronger transport axioms, e.g. unitary transport that carries the ray line to the ray line and the one-dimensional horizontal line to the horizontal line, then the residual dependence collapses to phase freedom on those lines, so only a diagonal phase action survives. Missing: no cited proof gives those stronger axioms for all admissible rules or proves Berry/Pancharatnam uniqueness here.

- **Dependencies**: One-parameter \(C^1\) pure-state ray curve; local lifts; first horizontal jet; endpoint nondegeneracy; a fixed gauge-covariant two-point transport rule; the explicit first-jet value/derivative block formulas; basic Hilbert-space linear algebra.
- **Strongest objection**: The counterexample uses only the minimal “gauge-covariant transport rule” language from the current notes. If the intended transport class is secretly narrower, the actual residual ambiguity may be smaller than full entrywise freedom. The present materials do not state or prove such a narrowing, so the safe verdict must remain the larger one.
- **Needed for promotion**: State the theorem in the note as: first-order bilateral whitening is a ray-curve-with-chosen-transport invariant, not a transport-independent matrix invariant. If a stronger statement is wanted, add and prove explicit transport axioms strong enough to show that different admissible rules differ only by a controlled diagonal phase, or prove uniqueness/canonicity of Berry/Pancharatnam transport in this exact first-jet setting.
