# gap-krylov-theorem

- **Claim**: For the exact unitary orbit \(\psi(t)=e^{-itH}\psi_0\), the ambient canonical subspace is an exact Krylov space:
  \[
  A_r(t)=\mathrm{span}\{\psi(t),\partial_t\psi(t),\dots,\partial_t^r\psi(t)\}
        =e^{-itH}\,\mathrm{span}\{\psi_0,H\psi_0,\dots,H^r\psi_0\}.
  \]
  So \(A_r(t)\) is precisely the order-\(r\) Krylov subspace for \((H,\psi_0)\), transported by the unitary flow.

  For the value-channel-removed space,
  \[
  O_r(t)=\mathrm{span}\{j_1(t),\dots,j_r(t)\},\qquad
  j_k(t)=P_t\,\partial_t^k\psi(t),\quad P_t=I-|\psi(t)\rangle\langle\psi(t)|,
  \]
  one has the exact identities
  \[
  O_r(t)=A_r(t)\cap \psi(t)^\perp
        =e^{-itH}\bigl(K_r(H,\psi_0)\cap \psi_0^\perp\bigr)
        =e^{-itH}\,\mathrm{span}\{P_0H\psi_0,\dots,P_0H^r\psi_0\}.
  \]
  This is not the ordinary Krylov space of the original pair \((H,\psi(t))\), but it is an exact compressed/shifted Krylov realization: if
  \[
  T_t:=P_tHP_t\big|_{\psi(t)^\perp},\qquad \eta_t:=P_tH\psi(t),
  \]
  then, whenever \(\eta_t\neq 0\),
  \[
  O_r(t)=\mathrm{span}\{\eta_t,T_t\eta_t,\dots,T_t^{\,r-1}\eta_t\}.
  \]
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Proved: from unitary evolution, \(\partial_t^k\psi(t)=(-iH)^k\psi(t)=e^{-itH}(-iH)^k\psi_0\). Hence \(A_r(t)\) is exactly the transported Krylov space \(e^{-itH}K_r(H,\psi_0)\). Proved: since \(P_t=e^{-itH}P_0e^{itH}\), one gets
  \[
  j_k(t)=P_t(-iH)^k\psi(t)=(-i)^k e^{-itH}P_0H^k\psi_0,
  \]
  so \(O_r(t)=e^{-itH}\mathrm{span}\{P_0H\psi_0,\dots,P_0H^r\psi_0\}\). Proved: because \(A_r=\mathrm{span}\{\psi\}\oplus O_r\), this gives \(O_r(t)=A_r(t)\cap \psi(t)^\perp\) exactly. Writing \(x_k:=P_0H^k\psi_0\) and \(T_0:=P_0HP_0|_{\psi_0^\perp}\), one has
  \[
  T_0x_k=x_{k+1}-\langle \psi_0,H^k\psi_0\rangle x_1,
  \]
  so \(\mathrm{span}\{x_1,\dots,x_r\}=\mathrm{span}\{x_1,T_0x_1,\dots,T_0^{r-1}x_1\}\). Conjugating by \(e^{-itH}\) gives the compressed Krylov formula for \(O_r(t)\).
- **Exact refs**: `quantum/paper/jet_gram_quantum_note.md:298-350`; `quantum/paper/jet_gram_quantum_note.md:316-380`; `quantum/findings.md:50-76`; `quantum/teams/20260423-201939-attack-krylov/notes/coordinator-brief.md:5-13`.
- **Dependencies**: Self-adjoint \(H\) with unitary flow \(e^{-itH}\); \(\psi_0\) in the domain of \(H^k\) up to order \(r\); the note’s definitions of \(A_r\), \(O_r\), and \(j_k\).
- **Strongest objection**: If “Krylov space” is interpreted strictly as the standard uncompressed space generated by repeatedly applying the original Hamiltonian to the original state, then only \(A_r(t)\) qualifies exactly. \(O_r(t)\) is exact only after either intersecting with \(\psi(t)^\perp\) or passing to the compressed pair \((P_tHP_t,\;P_tH\psi(t))\).
- **Needed for promotion**: A short writeup proving the two identities above, plus one explicit sentence in the note warning that \(O_r\) is not, in general, the ordinary Krylov space of \((H,\psi(t))\).
