# gap-a1-unification

- **Claim**: At first order, the singular values of the whitened first-horizontal-jet block are exactly the principal-angle cosines of the ambient planes `A_1(lambda)=span{psi(lambda),h_lambda}` in the identity/scalar-phase setting. For general transport conventions, they are instead the principal-angle cosines between `A_1(lambda_-)` and the transported plane `U A_1(lambda_+)`.
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Whitening the first-horizontal-jet block simply orthonormalizes the endpoint pairs `(psi,h)`, so the singular values are the principal-angle cosines of those planes in any common ambient realization. The exact ambient identification holds in the identity/scalar-phase class.
- **Exact refs**: `quantum/paper/jet_gram_quantum_note.md:181-194`; `quantum/findings.md:35-49`; `quantum/teams/20260423-140534-attack-a1-unification/notes/coordinator-brief.md:3-12`.
- **Dependencies**: The first-horizontal-jet theorem and the ambient `A_1` theorem.
- **Strongest objection**: Outside the scalar-phase/common-ambient setting, this is a transported-plane statement rather than a transport-free one.
- **Needed for promotion**: Already incorporated in the note in the appropriately scoped form.
