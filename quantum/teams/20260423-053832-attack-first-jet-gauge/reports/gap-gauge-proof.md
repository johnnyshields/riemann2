# gap-gauge-proof

- **Claim**: The fixed-local-lift theorem upgrades to a ray-space statement, but only relative to a chosen gauge-covariant transport rule along the given one-parameter ray curve. Let \(\lambda\mapsto [\psi(\lambda)]\) be a \(C^1\) one-parameter pure-state ray curve, choose any local lift \(|\psi(\lambda)\rangle\), set \(P(\lambda)=I-|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|\) and \(|j_\lambda\rangle=P(\lambda)\partial_\lambda|\psi(\lambda)\rangle\), assume \(\langle j_{\lambda_\pm}\mid j_{\lambda_\pm}\rangle>0\), and choose a two-point transport rule \(U_{-\leftarrow +}\) that is gauge-covariant under lift changes. Then the bilaterally whitened first-horizontal-jet block built from the frame \((|\psi\rangle,|j_\lambda\rangle)\) is independent of the choice of local lift, and together with the earlier result is also unchanged under orientation-preserving reparameterization. The exact surviving object is a ray-curve-with-transport invariant of the one-parameter first-horizontal-jet construction. It is not yet a transport-independent invariant of endpoints or ray space alone.
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Proved: the first horizontal jet is gauge-covariant at first order, since under \(|\psi\rangle\mapsto e^{i\chi(\lambda)}|\psi\rangle\) one has \(P\mapsto P\) and \(|j_\lambda\rangle\mapsto e^{i\chi(\lambda)}|j_\lambda\rangle\); the \(i\chi'|\psi\rangle\) term is killed by \(P\). Therefore the same-point block is lift-independent. Proved: if the transport rule is gauge-covariant, then transported endpoint vectors transform with the endpoint phases, so the mixed entries are unchanged under lift change; hence the mixed block is lift-independent. Proved: since both same-point and mixed blocks are lift-independent, the whitened block is lift-independent. Proved: the separate reparameterization theorem already shows exact invariance under orientation-preserving reparameterization. Conditional: Berry parallel transport gives the intended gauge-covariant rule only once the ray curve segment and its orientation are part of the data. Missing: any proof that different admissible transport rules give the same matrix, or that the matrix is determined by endpoints alone rather than by the chosen curve/transport.

- **Dependencies**: One-dimensional \(C^1\) pure-state ray curve; existence of local lifts; projector definition \(P=I-|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|\); first horizontal jet \(j_\lambda=P\partial_\lambda\psi\); endpoint nondegeneracy; a gauge-covariant two-point transport rule along the curve segment; orientation-preserving reparameterizations for the exact parameter-change statement.
- **Strongest objection**: This is not yet a canonical matrix invariant of ray space in the strongest sense. The proof only gives invariance under change of local lift once a transport convention has already been fixed on the ray curve. Berry transport is natural, but it is path/curve dependent and orientation sensitive; different transport conventions can change the matrix.
- **Needed for promotion**: Promote only the narrower statement: one-parameter first-horizontal-jet bilateral whitening is a well-defined invariant of a ray curve equipped with a gauge-covariant transport rule, and with Berry transport it is independent of local phase lift and orientation-preserving reparameterization. To promote further, prove either transport-independence, or a theorem that Berry transport is the unique admissible canonical rule in this setting, or else weaken the promoted object to transported horizontal-jet subspace data.
