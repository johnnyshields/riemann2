## Absorbed Findings

- Source identification: the RH-side local object is the whitened local jet
  cross-Gram block built from same-point jet Gram matrices and a mixed cross
  block, with source anchors in `paper/proof_of_rh.tex:149-259`.
- First-order bridge: the same-point horizontal derivative Gram matrix of a
  normalized pure-state family is exactly the quantum geometric tensor.
- First-horizontal two-point bridge: after fixing the transport rule, the
  bilaterally whitened first-jet block is invariant under
  orientation-preserving one-parameter reparameterization; orientation reversal
  acts by derivative-channel sign conjugation.
- Higher raw projected coordinate jets transform by invertible upper-triangular
  changes under phase gauge and reparameterization. Raw matrices fail, while
  `O_r = span{j_1,...,j_r}` survives.
- Berry-covariant one-parameter jets and ordered/symmetrized multiparameter
  covariant jets generate the same canonical `O_r` filtrations as raw mixed
  partials.
- Endpoint osculating subspaces give canonical principal angles, projector
  compressions, cross-contractions, polar partial isometries, and simple-
  spectrum principal frames when the nonzero spectrum is simple.
- Repeated principal values do not determine canonical principal frames from
  the subspace pair alone; only the block partial isometry is canonical.
- Fixed endpoint subspaces determine orthonormal-frame comparison matrices only
  up to one biunitary orbit, classified by the singular values/principal-angle
  cosines.
- Multiparameter jet hierarchies determine the `O_r`/`A_r` filtrations and
  operator/principal-angle package, but not an ordered matrix or frame from
  ray-field data alone.
- The exact relation `A_r = span{psi} \oplus O_r` is a role split:
  `O_r` is the horizontal/value-channel-removed object and `A_r` is the ambient
  endpoint-subspace extension.
- Coherent reflection access to endpoint projectors gives a phase-estimation
  protocol for the principal-angle spectrum through
  `(2Pi_- - I)(2Pi_+ - I)`.
- Qutrit, quartit, Veronese, spherical-twist, and first-order finite-jet
  genericity results show that surviving `A_r`/`O_r` data can be richer than
  overlap alone.
- Unitary/Krylov, commutator/leakage, short-time expansion, and Kato-transport
  specializations remain useful interpretations but not stronger endpoint
  matrix invariants.
