# gap-matrix-vs-subspace

- **Claim**: For a future `quantum/` draft, the honest invariant should be framed at the subspace level, not as a canonical matrix. What survives the gauge and reparameterization objections is: at first order, same-point horizontal-jet Gram data; at higher order, the osculating subspaces generated by covariant horizontal jets; and at two points, after inserting endpoint transport, the singular values or principal angles between the corresponding orthonormal jet subspaces. A genuinely matrix-valued object survives only with extra structure fixing all of: an intrinsic parameter, a higher-order covariant-jet convention, a canonical transport between endpoints, and a canonical frame/trivialization of each jet subspace. Without that extra structure, the matrix is presentation-dependent.
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Proved: the paper-side object is explicitly a bilateral whitening of same-point and mixed jet Gram blocks, so any quantum analogue must explain why those matrix entries are basis-independent rather than merely convenient coordinates. Proved: the current quantum analysis shows the first horizontal derivative is gauge-covariant, so its same-point norm/Gram data is gauge invariant, but higher projected coordinate jets are not covariant; they mix with lower jets under phase gauge and also under reparameterization. Proved: bilateral whitening is not invariant under those non-orthogonal triangular jet-coordinate changes, so the raw whitened matrix cannot be canonical on ray space. Proved: mixed two-point overlaps are not gauge invariant unless one inserts transport, so even first-order two-point matrices need extra transport data. Conditional: if one chooses a specific intrinsic parameter, a specific higher-order covariant derivative scheme, Berry/Pancharatnam-type transport, and orthonormal frames at both endpoints, then one can write a matrix of transported frame overlaps; but its invariant content is then only its unitary-orbit data, concretely singular values/principal angles, unless a further theorem canonically fixes the frames. Missing: a proof that any such frame choice is canonical in the intended quantum families, and a worked asymmetric example showing matrix entries carrying invariant information beyond those subspace invariants.
- **Exact refs**: `paper/proof_of_rh.tex:173-215`; `paper/proof_of_rh.tex:226-259`; `quantum/findings.md:33-40`; `quantum/findings.md:48-53`; `quantum/20260423-043347-research-team-jet-gram-qm/notes/synthesis-1.md:14-25`; `quantum/20260423-043347-research-team-jet-gram-qm/reports/gap-closer-gauge.md:3-9`; `quantum/20260423-043347-research-team-jet-gram-qm/reports/verifier-adversarial.md:3-9`; `quantum/20260423-051725-attack-jet-qgt-core/notes/coordinator-brief.md:9-15`.
- **Dependencies**: Standard ray-space geometry for normalized pure states modulo phase, the projector-based horizontal derivative at first order, Berry/Pancharatnam parallel transport for two-point comparison, chain-rule/gauge-mixing behavior of higher derivatives, and the linear-algebra fact that whitening is canonical only up to orthogonal or unitary frame changes, not arbitrary triangular coordinate changes.
- **Strongest objection**: The no-matrix verdict may be too pessimistic if a natural higher-order quantum connection exists whose covariant jet frames are canonically selected by the physics, so that the transported overlap matrix itself, not just its singular values, becomes invariant. None of the cited materials proves such a frame theorem, but without ruling it out completely, the conclusion should be stated as “no canonical matrix has been justified so far,” not “no canonical matrix can ever exist.”
- **Needed for promotion**: A future positive matrix-valued claim would need exact specification and proof of the extra structure: one intrinsic parameter choice, one higher-order covariant-jet definition, one canonical endpoint transport rule, and one canonical orthonormal frame selection theorem for each jet subspace. Short of that, the draft should be written around a subspace invariant: transported horizontal-jet subspaces with singular values/principal angles as the honest two-point data, and first-order reduction to Fubini-Study/QGT/Berry as the clean established bridge.
