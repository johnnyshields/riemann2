# verifier-adversarial

- **Claim**: The lower-order Veronese direction `r < n-1` is not currently worth many more cycles as a primary line. The honest proved payoff in the present record is the complement-basis theorem above; beyond that, returns look flattening unless a new mechanism appears.
- **Status**: proved
- **Evidence**: Proved: the note and findings already isolate the exact family-level payoff at top order, where `A_{n-1}` is a codimension-`1` hyperplane with explicit normal and `O_{n-1}` has an explicit codimension-`2` complement-plane description; both reports state that no equally clean exact closed formula is justified for general lower orders `r < n-1`. Proved: the new lower-order result gives an exact complement basis, but not a comparably sharp all-`(n,r)` principal-angle or singular-value formula. Conditional: lower orders may still hide useful recurrences or weaker invariants, but from current scope alone that is a side question, not the next main theorem.
- **Exact refs**: `quantum/findings.md:153-166`; `quantum/paper/jet_gram_quantum_note.md:343-363`; `quantum/paper/jet_gram_quantum_note.md:574-590`; `quantum/20260423-195245-attack-lower-order-veronese/reports/gap-lower-order-veronese.md:3-17`; `quantum/20260423-195245-attack-lower-order-veronese/notes/coordinator-brief.md:5-14`.
- **Dependencies**: The already proved top-order Veronese theorems for `A_{n-1}` and `O_{n-1}`; the current findings ledger; the assumption that “worth more cycles” means “likely to produce a new exact promotable statement rather than more benchmark algebra.”
- **Strongest objection**: This may be too pessimistic: lower-order Veronese spaces often have structured annihilators and may admit representation-theoretic or finite-difference descriptions not tried here, so from complement-basis scope alone one cannot prove the direction is exhausted.
- **Needed for promotion**: Keep the lower-order theorem in the archive and findings as a side corollary. Reopen only if a new mechanism appears for turning the complement basis into a useful canonical spectral formula.
