# Evidence

## Proved / Source-Supported

1. **Native terminology and non-equivalence.** The paper defines the one-pair
   fixed-sector data \(A_5^{\mathfrak f}\), \(A_7^{\mathfrak f}\), and
   \(\Delta_7\) at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:7004-7062`. It then proves the
   one-pair "Quotient-septic closure" theorem at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:7892-7924`:
   \([A_7^{\mathfrak f}]=[A_{7,K_1}^{\mathfrak f}]\) in
   \(\mathfrak f/\mathbf C A_5^{\mathfrak f}\) and
   \(\Delta_7=\Delta_{7,K_1}\). The remark at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:7976-8001` says this closes only
   the one-pair canonical quotient level and leaves raw representatives
   noncanonical. By contrast, the two-atom septic quotient defect is introduced
   separately at `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:11603-11615` as
   \(\overline E_{12}^{(7;1)}\) in the moving
   \(h_1\)-quotient. Therefore the first-wave warning is source-supported:
   do not reuse "quotient-septic closure" as the name of UV-010.

2. **Good-patch determinant quotient trivialization.** The determinant
   coordinate is source-supported. The paper states the septic gauge law
   \((A_7^{\mathfrak f})^{\mathrm{new}}=
   A_7^{\mathfrak f}+c_2A_5^{\mathfrak f}\) at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:7065-7084` and proves
   determinant invariance at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:7157-7191`. For the two-atom
   finite-delta representative \(R\), the paper explicitly uses
   \(\det(R,A_{5,1}^{\mathfrak f})\) and proves representative independence at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:11705-11745` and
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:11768-11774`. The first-wave local
   trivialization from the moving quotient to the midpoint quotient is not a
   named paper lemma, but it is the standard linear-algebra consequence of these
   source lines on \(A_5^{\mathfrak f}(m)\neq0\): the kernel of
   \(R\mapsto\det(R,A_5^{\mathfrak f}(m))\) is exactly
   \(\mathbf C A_5^{\mathfrak f}(m)\).

3. **Centered \(D_2\).** The source exposes the centered determinant channel.
   The centered Taylor package at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:23294-23409` gives
   \(u_5,v_5,u_{7,K_1},v_{7,K_1}\), defines
   \(\Delta_7(s)=D_2s^2+D_4s^4+D_6s^6+\cdots\), and states
   \(D_2=\widehat U_1V_1-U_1\widehat V_1\). This supports carrying \(D_2\) as a
   comparison or pressure target.

4. **Downstream separation.** The later queue summaries keep finite-order
   source merger, quotient-diagonal collapse, quotient transport/state-local
   closure, and package-level coincidence separate. See
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:24520-24610` and
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:24990-25030`.

## Conditional

1. **Normalized fixed-target theorem.** The finite-delta theorem supports the
   \(a_1^{-1}\) normalization only conditionally: it chooses \(R\) as a
   representative of
   \(-a_1^{-1}\overline E_{12}^{(7;1)}\) at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:11625-11638`. The edge-template
   shape is also conditional: if an actual corrected defect \(E\) is analytic,
   swap-even, and collision-vanishing, then
   \(E=\delta^2\mathcal H(m,\kappa,\delta^2)\) at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:12452-12469`. Combining these two
   is source-compatible, but only after the actual order-7 quotient defect is a
   fixed-target analytic section.

2. **\([R]_{\mathrm{edge}}\) boundary.** The formula
   \([R]_{\mathrm{edge}}=-\mathcal H_7^q(m,\kappa,0)\) is not an existing
   source result. It is a valid proposed definition only if
   \(\overline E_{12}^{(7;1)}\) has first been normalized by \(a_1^{-1}\),
   moved to the midpoint quotient, and proved to have the
   \(\delta^2\mathcal H_7^q\) edge form. The source lines support the
   ingredients separately, not the final object.

3. **Using the source criterion.** The finite-order source criterion at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:11888-11932` proves only a generic
   interaction factorization
   \(\mathcal I_2=a_1a_2(h_1-h_2)^2\mathcal J_2\) after three hypotheses.
   Its order-7 corollary at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:11994-12040` gives only
   \(\overline E_{12}^{(7;1)}=O(\delta^2)\), and only after assuming the actual
   corrected package satisfies the source criterion.

## Missing

1. **No non-merger order-7 edge law.** The source criterion imports diagonal
   merger as a hypothesis at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:11911-11913`, and the paper repeats
   that the remaining finite-order source-level input includes diagonal merger
   at `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:12168-12189`. That cannot prove
   UV-010 in its current no-merger form.

2. **No order-7 quotient analogue in the collision chart.** The
   collision-cancellation chart gives the variables \(m,\delta,\lambda,\omega\)
   and \(\kappa=2\omega/\delta\) at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:12385-12413`, then displays only
   cubic/quintic edge laws at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:12477-12510`. There is no displayed
   septic quotient law for \(\overline E_{12}^{(7;1)}\).

3. **No actual \(\mathcal J_2^{(7)}\) / \(\mathfrak O_7\).** Source search in
   `rh/proof_of_rh.tex` produced no definition of `H_7`, `\mathcal H_7`,
   `H_7^q`, `\mathfrak O_7`, or `\mathcal J_2^{(7)}`. The source has generic
   \(\mathcal J_2\) at `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:11920-11930`
   and centered \(D_2\) at
   `C:/workspace/riemann2/rh/proof_of_rh.tex:23402`, but no actual
   two-atom order-7 quotient Hessian.

4. **No source prohibition of the septic freedom.** No audited line forbids an
   additional septic quotient edge contribution before actual package
   construction. The negative formal \(a_1a_2\delta^2P(m,\kappa)\) model from
   the prior gap-closer remains a valid pressure test against any proof that
   uses only lower edge laws, the abstract source criterion, or determinant
   scalarization.
