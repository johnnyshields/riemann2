# L2YR0 / L0YR2 Homogeneous Order Verdict

## Proved Under The Homogeneous Scalar Grade

Assume
\[
\operatorname{Gr}_a r
=\frac{r^{(a+2)}(m)}{(a+2)!}(t-m)^{a+2}.
\]
The same-point source-linear block contains the \(g=r''\) channel, so a
grade-\(a\) same-point input begins at ordinary \(z\)-order \(a\).

The mixed input has the exact transpose parity \(M(-z)=M(z)^T\).  For the odd
homogeneous pieces relevant here,
\[
M^{[1]}\ \text{starts at order }2,
\qquad
M^{[5]}\ \text{starts at order }6.
\]

The holomorphic second Frechet term of \(G^{(0)}(z)^{-1/2}\) cannot lower
ordinary \(z\)-order: if two same-point inputs begin at orders \(a\) and \(b\),
then the second-Frechet output begins at order at least \(a+b\).  This follows
from holomorphy of the inverse square-root functional calculus and the
nonnegative-power \(z\)-series of the baseline block.

## L2YR0 / L0YR2 Terms

The \(L_2YR_0\) and mirror \(L_0YR_2\) grade placements of type \((1,1,5)\)
are:

1. second-Frechet grades \(\{1,1\}\) with \(M^{[5]}\):
\[
2+6+0=8;
\]
2. second-Frechet grades \(\{1,5\}\) with \(M^{[1]}\):
\[
6+2+0=8.
\]

The baseline \(L_0\) or \(R_0\) factor has order \(0\), so neither family
has a \([z^7]\) term.  Thus both \(L_2YR_0\) and \(L_0YR_2\) are absent at
\(B_7^{\mathfrak f}\) under the homogeneous convention.

## Remaining Caveat

This is an order-count closure, not a determinant identity.  It depends on the
homogeneous scalar-grade convention.  If UV-026 later chooses a matrix-output
or parity-corrected non-homogeneous definition of \(M^{[5]}\), the
\(L_2YR_0/L_0YR_2\) coefficient theorem must be reopened.
