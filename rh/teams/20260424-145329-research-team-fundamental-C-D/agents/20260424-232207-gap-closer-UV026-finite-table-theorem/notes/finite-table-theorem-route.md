## Stage 2: Frechet Table Algebra

This part is formally derivable once Stage 1 is available.

For each sign, work in the finite ring generated by \(z\), tags, and finite
grades, modulo \(z^8\) and homogeneous whitening order \(>3\).  Let
\[
G_\pm(\epsilon,z)=G_\pm^{(0)}(z)+E_\pm(\epsilon,z)
\]
be the Stage 1 same-point input.  Define
\[
S_\pm(\epsilon,z)^2=G_\pm(\epsilon,z),
\qquad
B_\pm(\epsilon,z)S_\pm(\epsilon,z)=I,
\]
with \(S_{\pm,0}=G_\pm^{(0)1/2}\) and
\(B_{\pm,0}=G_\pm^{(0)-1/2}\).  Since the baseline is positive definite, the
constant Sylvester map
\[
X\mapsto S_{\pm,0}X+XS_{\pm,0}
\]
is invertible.  Coefficients of \(S_\pm\) and \(B_\pm\) through \(z^7\) and
homogeneous whitening order \(3\) are therefore uniquely determined by finite
linear algebra.

The output tables are:

- first Frechet:
  \[
  \Lambda_{i,\pm}^{[1]}=D(G_\pm^{(0)-1/2})[H_{i,\pm}^{[1]}],
  \qquad
  \Lambda_{i,\pm}^{[5]}=D(G_\pm^{(0)-1/2})[H_{i,\pm}^{[5]}];
  \]
- second Frechet:
  \[
  \mathcal L_{\pm,ij}^{\{1,1\}},\qquad
  \mathcal L_{\pm,ij}^{\{1,5\}};
  \]
- third Frechet:
  \[
  \mathcal L_{\pm,ijk}^{(1,1,5)}.
  \]

These can be read either as Frechet derivatives or as the relevant homogeneous
coefficients of \(B_\pm(\epsilon,z)\).  This avoids relying on a generic
Loewner witness: the proof is by the finite equations \(S^2=G\) and
\(BS=I\).

## Minimal Paper-Ready Theorem Shape

The shortest useful theorem is not "the tables exist."  It is:

**Finite normalized source-table theorem.**  In the Stage 0 normalization, the
tagged block \(\mathcal B_2\) has the Stage 1 coefficient tables through
order \(7\).  The tables are obtained by expanding the exact corrected
same-point and mixed block formulas in the finite quotient \(z^8=0\), after
removing the mixed-block singularities.  Applying the Stage 2 finite
square-root algebra gives the first-, second-, and third-Frechet tables.  The
Stage 3 gate vectors for every non-\((1,1)\), finite-grade \((1,1,5)\) cubic
family are then explicit finite sums in these tables.

This theorem supplies the data.  UV-026 still needs the subsequent determinant
identities unless the theorem also includes the Stage 3 proportionality
checks.
