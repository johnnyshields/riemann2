## Claim

Continuity plus diagonal collapse of the **actual corrected quotient odd germ** is too weak, from the present draft alone, to imply Bottleneck C. That route can exclude exact close two-point bad cores on compact subsets of `\{c\neq 0\}`; it does not determine the corrected reduced package on the blow-up exceptional divisor. To reach Bottleneck C one must strengthen the input from scalar quotient-germ collapse to a **package-level diagonal-merger / collision-functoriality statement** for the corrected reduced package, which is essentially the Bottleneck C theorem itself.
