## Why the relative \((\log X)^{2j}\) estimate fails

The standard short-interval mean-square estimate gives an **absolute coefficient-side bound**, schematically

\[
\int_I\left|\sum_{n\le N} c_n n^{-it}\right|^2dt
\ll
(|I|+N)\sum_{n\le N}|c_n|^2.
\]

After differentiating \(j\) times, it gives something like

\[
\int_I |F_X^{(j)}(t)|^2dt
\ll
(|I|+X)(\log X)^{2j}
\sum_n |c_n|^2.
\]

It does **not** imply

\[
\int_I |F_X^{(j)}|^2
\ll
(\log X)^{2j}\int_I |F_X|^2,
\]

because \(\int_I|F_X|^2\) may be much smaller than the coefficient square-sum.

A simple model shows the obstruction. Let

\[
F(t)=1-e^{-i\delta t},\qquad I=[-\ell,\ell],\qquad W_{\ref}=1.
\]

For \(\delta\ell\ll1\),

\[
F(t)=i\delta t+O(\delta^2t^2),
\]

so

\[
\int_I |F(t)|^2dt\asymp \delta^2\ell^3,
\]

while

\[
F'(t)=i\delta e^{-i\delta t},
\qquad
\int_I |F'(t)|^2dt\asymp \delta^2\ell.
\]

Therefore

\[
\frac{\int_I |F'(t)|^2dt}{\int_I |F(t)|^2dt}
\asymp \ell^{-2}.
\]

If \(\ell\ll1/\log X\), this ratio is much larger than \((\log X)^2\). This is exactly the local-cancellation problem flagged in the instruction. A two-frequency Dirichlet polynomial can cancel to first order on a packet while its derivative remains non-negligible.

So the line in the proof saying that the standard mean-square theorem gives the relative bound is the invalid step.
