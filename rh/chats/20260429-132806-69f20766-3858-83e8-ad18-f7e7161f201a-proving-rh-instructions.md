## 4. Core finite inequality: charge-zero anti-lock

### Lemma 1: one-step shift lower bound

Let \(F(z)\) be a Hilbert-valued polynomial of degree at most \(N\),

\[
F(z)=\sum_{k=0}^{N}f_kz^k.
\]

Then for every \(\lambda\in\mathbb C\),

\[
\|(1-\lambda z)F\|_{L^2(\mathbb T)}
\ge
\frac{1}{N+1}\|F\|_{L^2(\mathbb T)}.
\]

Proof.

Write

\[
Y(z)=(1-\lambda z)F(z)
=
\sum_{k=0}^{N+1}y_kz^k.
\]

Thus

\[
y_0=f_0,
\]

\[
y_k=f_k-\lambda f_{k-1}
\qquad
1\le k\le N,
\]

and

\[
y_{N+1}=-\lambda f_N.
\]

If \(|\lambda|\le1\), then the recurrence gives

\[
f_k
=
\sum_{j=0}^{k}\lambda^j y_{k-j}.
\]

Hence

\[
\|f_k\|
\le
\sum_{j=0}^{k}\|y_{k-j}\|.
\]

The convolution kernel has \(\ell^1\)-norm at most \(N+1\), so Young’s inequality gives

\[
\|F\|_{\ell^2}
\le
(N+1)\|Y\|_{\ell^2}.
\]

Since coefficient \(\ell^2\)-norm equals \(L^2(\mathbb T)\)-norm,

\[
\|F\|_{L^2(\mathbb T)}
\le
(N+1)\|(1-\lambda z)F\|_{L^2(\mathbb T)}.
\]

If \(|\lambda|>1\), solve backwards:

\[
f_k
=
-\sum_{j=k+1}^{N+1}\lambda^{-(j-k)}y_j.
\]

Again the convolution kernel has \(\ell^1\)-norm at most \(N+1\), because \(|\lambda|^{-1}<1\). Thus the same estimate holds.

Therefore

\[
\|(1-\lambda z)F\|_{L^2(\mathbb T)}
\ge
\frac1{N+1}\|F\|_{L^2(\mathbb T)}.
\]
