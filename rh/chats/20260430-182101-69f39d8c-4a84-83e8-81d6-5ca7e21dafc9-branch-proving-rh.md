## 2. Ordinary zero-separated strip existence

There are two possible meanings of “ordinary zero-separated Hardy source strip.”

### Weak interpretation

A weak version says: near height \(\gamma\), one can find some interval on the critical line avoiding zeros of \(\zeta(1/2+it)\).

This is essentially true in a soft pigeonhole sense. If one takes an interval

\[
I=[\gamma-H,\gamma+H],
\]

then the zero-counting formula gives roughly

\[
N(I)\ll H\log \gamma+\log \gamma
\]

critical-line zeros in \(I\), counting multiplicity, so there is some subinterval of length at least about

\[
\gg \frac{H}{H\log \gamma+\log\gamma}.
\]

For fixed or moderate \(H\), this gives a gap of order at best

\[
\gg (\log \gamma)^{-1}
\]

in a local averaging sense.

This is not enough for the draft’s branch-entry theorem, because it does not say that the selected strip carries the off-line-zero obstruction.

### Strong interpretation

The strong version needed by the draft is closer to:

\[
\text{the off-line zero at height }\gamma
\text{ forces an ordinary zero-separated source strip containing the correct obstruction packet,}
\]

with polynomially controlled constants in the canonical scale \(Q=Q(\gamma)\).

This does **not** follow from standard zero-counting, zero-density, or functional-equation symmetry.

The obstacle is that the off-line zero has horizontal displacement

\[
\eta=\left|\beta-\tfrac12\right|,
\]

and there is no standard lower bound of the form

\[
\eta\ge \gamma^{-C}
\]

for a hypothetical off-line zero. If \(\eta\) is much smaller than any negative power of \(\gamma\), then any construction whose constants depend on resolving the quartet at horizontal scale \(\eta\) cannot have losses bounded by \(Q(\gamma)^{-C}\) unless \(Q\) also includes \(\eta^{-1}\).

But the assigned theorem states \(Q=Q(\gamma)\), not \(Q=Q(\gamma,\eta)\). That is too weak.
