## Claim

The smallest honest theorem milestone that counts as a real non-zeta result, from current scope alone, is the following primitive-Dirichlet paired source-plus-slot theorem.

**Proposed milestone theorem.** Let \(\chi\) be a primitive nonprincipal Dirichlet character, and define
\[
\Xi_\chi(s)=\Lambda(s,\chi)\Lambda(s,\bar\chi),
\qquad
\Phi_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(s)=\frac{\Xi_\chi(2s-1)}{\Xi_\chi(2s)}.
\]
Then there exist explicitly defined paired quantities \(q_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m)\) and \(B_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m)\), with
\[
S_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m)=q_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m)-B_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m),
\]
such that \(S_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m)\) is exactly the coefficient occupying the paired local value-channel slot in a decomposition
\[
\Delta_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}} = S_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}} A^{\mathrm{pair}}_{\mathrm{val},\chi} + R_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}.
\]

What it would claim:

- an exact non-zeta source object built from primitive Dirichlet completed \(L\)-functions;
- an exact identification of the paired scalar with the manuscript-style local value-channel coefficient;
- a realized non-zeta theorem-level interface to the existing local coefficient algebra.

What it would not claim:

- no GRH, ERH, or zero-free-region consequence;
- no remainder dominance such as \(\Psi_{x,d}(R)=o((x/B)S)\) or \(S_2=o(xQ^2S)\);
- no denominator comparability, corrected whitening, odd/transverse realization, or boundary theorem;
- no claim that positivity or final majorization has already been transported downstairs beyond the exact paired slot.
