# Claim

Among primitive Dirichlet subclasses, real/self-dual characters are cleaner only at the front end: they remove the non-self-dual channel-choice ambiguity and make a one-channel real scalar more plausible. They do not presently overtake the paired-complex `((\chi,\bar\chi))` route as the safest first non-zeta theorem target, because the remaining burden after pairing is already dominated by exact source-to-slot identification, background unification, convergence/regularization, denominator comparability, whitening, and odd/transverse transport rather than by root-number or phase ambiguity alone. From current scope alone, the paired-complex route remains the more relevant first non-zeta theorem target, while real/self-dual characters are only a cleaner candidate-selection subclass.

# Evidence

Proved from the supplied notes and prior verifier boundary:

- `dirichlet_paired_source.md` is explicit that pairing fixes only front-end issues: scalar reality/sign safety, cleaner boundary-phase packaging, and removal of the residual root number. The same note is equally explicit that pairing does not fix the theorem-sized source gap, local-slot identification, denominator comparability, corrected whitening, odd/transverse realization, or boundary control.
- `quotient_phase.md` and `universal_source_kernel.md` sharpen the same split. The quotient gives only a phase-channel upgrade, and the one-zero kernel is universal only upstairs once a family-specific quotient/source theorem exists. The manuscript's actual `S(m)` slot, its background identification, and compact-interval convergence remain family-specific.
- Earlier paired-source and paired-background reports already isolate the smallest safe paired target: an exact paired-scalar source theorem with full bookkeeping and exact local-slot identification. Those reports also warn that pairing does not materially shrink the theorem burden once exact `S(m)`-slot realization is demanded.
- The portability verifier and local-package report separate self-dual/real cases from genuinely complex non-self-dual ones, but they also state that real/self-dual cleanliness is only a symmetry-level advantage. The analytic realization package is still missing even there: one still needs a real sign-compatible calibration amplitude, microscopic denominator comparability, preserved whitening scales, and a corrected odd holomorphic transverse scalar.
- The project-level ranking still names the primitive Dirichlet paired-scalar source theorem as the best first non-zeta realization target from current scope alone. No supplied note replaces that ranking by a real-character-only theorem target.

Conditional on [restricting primitive Dirichlet to real/self-dual characters]:

- Channel selection becomes simpler. One avoids the extra decision between a rotated single channel, a paired `((\chi,\bar\chi))` scalar, or a matrix fallback.
- The front-end object is more plausible as a real scalar source from the start, so hidden background from residual root-number and conjugate-pair packaging is reduced.
- Even in that narrower subclass, the load-bearing theorem is still exact scalar-slot realization. The notes do not show that self-duality alone proves the exact background-subtracted scalar occupying the manuscript's `S(m)` slot, nor that it rebuilds the denominator, whitening, remainder, and odd-package layers.

Missing:

- A theorem for real primitive characters identifying one canonical completed-object scalar whose background-subtracted part is exactly the manuscript's local `S(m)`-slot coefficient.
- A proof that the self-dual subclass automatically preserves the zeta-style microscopic denominator architecture, compact-interval convergence/regularization, corrected whitening gain, and odd/transverse scalar package.
- Any roadmap note showing that, once these hidden local-package requirements are counted explicitly, the real-character theorem is shorter than the paired-complex exact-source theorem.
- Any evidence that the paired-complex route's current top ranking was meant to exclude the self-dual primitive subclass. The existing ranking is phrased at the primitive-Dirichlet family level and still points to the paired theorem.

Honest verdict: real/self-dual primitive characters do simplify channel choice and reduce hidden front-end clutter, but current notes do not show that this simplification dominates the remaining source-to-slot theorem burden. The paired-complex route remains the more relevant first non-zeta theorem target from current scope alone. Real characters are best read as a cleaner conditional test subclass, not yet as the theorem that overtakes the paired target.

# Strongest objection

The strongest objection is that the report may be undercounting how much the real/self-dual subclass collapses hidden bookkeeping. If self-duality not only removes phase ambiguity but also forces the correct background normalization, a real sign-compatible scalar amplitude, and the same local denominator and odd-package architecture, then the real-character theorem could be strictly shorter than the paired-complex theorem. Current notes do not prove that collapse. They support only the narrower claim that self-duality cleans up channel choice and front-end symmetry.
