# gap-paired-patch-order-routeA

- **Claim**

  From current scope alone, the cleanest safe theorem-facing patch order is a localized 2-step program, not a collapsed 1-step theorem and not a theorem-facing 3-step chain.

  Minimal sequence:

  1. prove a family-specific compact-interval paired source-package theorem for
     \(
     \Phi_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(s)=\Xi_\chi(2s-1)/\Xi_\chi(2s),
     \quad
     \Xi_\chi(s)=\Lambda(s,\chi)\Lambda(s,\bar\chi),
     \)
     with explicit local regularization, unified paired background, and multiplicity bookkeeping;
  2. prove a separate exact local patch-to-slot identification theorem showing that the localized background-subtracted scalar is exactly the manuscript's paired \(S(m)\) coefficient in the value channel.

  Safe wording: compact localization supplies the right theorem-facing scaffold, but exact paired \(S(m)\)-slot realization is still a separate missing theorem burden. Any 3-step order is already mixing in downstream transport tasks rather than the paired theorem-facing patch itself.

- **Status**

  open

- **Evidence**

  **proved**

  The supplied notes consistently separate the paired target into an upstairs source realization and a downstairs exact local-slot identification. The paired-slot note states this split explicitly (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/paired_slot_realization.md:11-24`). The compact-theorem note says the right first theorem-facing shape is a compact-interval blueprint and that this buys a zeta-like 2-step theorem-facing shape, while keeping exact paired \(S(m)\)-slot realization and unified background/convergence/multiplicity as explicit missing burdens (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/paired_compact_theorem.md:8-27`). The paired-source note agrees that pairing cleans the object but does not by itself fix the theorem-sized source gap or local-slot identification (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/dirichlet_paired_source.md:49-56`, `:66-87`). The quotient-phase note only upgrades the phase channel, not the realized positive \(S(m)\)-analogue or leading value-channel coefficient theorem (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/quotient_phase.md:25-37`).

  The adversarial compact-theorem report makes the patch order explicit: first a compact-interval paired source theorem, then a separate exact local patch-to-slot identification theorem (`tasks/20260424-020505-attack-gap-paired-compact-theorem/reports/verifier-adversarial-paired-compact-theorem.md:5-13`). Route B from the same attack says the 2-step split is the safe blueprint and warns that compact localization does not make the exact paired slot automatic (`tasks/20260424-020505-attack-gap-paired-compact-theorem/reports/gap-paired-compact-routeB.md:5-10`, `:31-41`).

  **conditional on [a sufficiently sharp compact-interval paired source theorem]**

  If step 1 is formulated strongly enough, it may absorb some of the bookkeeping now listed under step 2, especially background packaging details. But current scope does not justify collapsing step 2 entirely. The strongest safe conditional statement is that compact localization gives the right local scaffold for the paired theorem-facing patch, and then exact slot identification attaches that local scalar to the manuscript coefficient (`tasks/20260424-020505-attack-gap-paired-compact-theorem/reports/verifier-adversarial-paired-compact-theorem.md:32-42`; `tasks/20260423-194735-attack-gap-paired-slot-realization/reports/verifier-adversarial-paired-slot-realization.md:3-8`, `:27-39`).

  **missing**

  What is not proved from current scope is any collapse to a 1-step theorem. Also missing is any reason to promote a theorem-facing 3-step order for the paired patch itself. A 3-step sequence only appears once downstream tasks are included, such as remainder dominance or corrected odd/transverse transport, but those are outside the theorem-facing paired patch target (`tasks/20260423-194735-attack-gap-paired-slot-realization/reports/gap-paired-slot-routeB.md:26-30`). At the paired patch level, the missing items are still theorem-sized: compact-interval convergence/regularization, unified paired background, explicit multiplicity bookkeeping, and exact proof that the resulting scalar is the manuscript's paired \(S(m)\) slot rather than a nearby positive scalar (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/paired_compact_theorem.md:23-27`; `tasks/20260424-020505-attack-gap-paired-compact-theorem/reports/verifier-adversarial-paired-compact-theorem.md:36-42`).

  Honest verdict: the safe theorem-facing patch order is 2-step local. Step 1 is the compact-interval paired source package. Step 2 is exact patch-to-slot identification. Anything shorter overclaims, and anything longer is mixing in downstream non-patch burdens.

- **Dependencies**

  - a family-specific compact-interval theorem for `\Phi_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}` with explicit regularization;
  - one theorem-facing unified paired background term covering conductor/scaling, gamma-derivative, and any trivial-zero or pole corrections;
  - explicit multiplicity bookkeeping at the localized level;
  - exact proof that the localized background-subtracted scalar equals the manuscript's paired `S(m)` coefficient;
  - continued separation from downstream remainder, odd/transverse, denominator/whitening, and boundary tasks.

- **Strongest objection**

  A sufficiently sharp compact-interval theorem might force the background package and slot identification together, so the 2-step order could be overly cautious as a workflow description. Current scope does not prove that collapse. From the supplied notes, treating the two layers as already fused would be overclaim.

- **Needed for promotion**

  - state the compact-interval paired source-package theorem with exact hypotheses, local variables, singularity exclusions, regularization, and multiplicity conventions;
  - prove the unified paired background term in theorem-facing form rather than prose-level bookkeeping;
  - prove exact equality between the localized background-subtracted scalar and the manuscript's paired `S(m)` slot;
  - adversarially check that no branch, factor-of-`2`, or trivial-zero correction is hidden in the patch-to-slot step;
  - keep the claim scoped to `2-step localized paired patch order` until those items are closed.
