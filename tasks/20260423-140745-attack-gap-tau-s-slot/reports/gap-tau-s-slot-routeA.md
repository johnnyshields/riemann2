Claim

For self-dual tau, a quotient-source theorem would make the route to the exact positive `S(m)` slot cleaner than in the primitive complex Dirichlet case, but only in a narrow sense: self-duality removes the phase-selection and scalar-vs-paired-channel ambiguity, not the family-specific realization work. Tau is therefore the cleanest exact-`S`-slot candidate in the current project only conditionally on a theorem that outputs the exact background-subtracted scalar in the manuscript's slot. Unconditionally, tau is only the cleanest phase-channel candidate.

Status

open

Evidence

Proved:

- The completed quotient `phi_F(s)=Lambda_F(2s-1)/Lambda_F(2s)` is a serious strip-edge phase candidate, and for self-dual tau the notes rank it as the cleanest current phase candidate.
- Conditional on a family-specific quotient/source theorem, the one-zero strip-edge quotient-log-derivative kernel and its positivity are universal upstairs.
- If a family theorem yields the exact scalar occupying the manuscript's `S(m)` slot, then the manuscript already supplies the leading-channel coefficient theorem; no new coefficient theorem of the form `Delta = S A_val + R` is needed.

Conditional on [family-specific quotient/source theorem with exact scalar identification]:

- Tau is cleaner than primitive complex Dirichlet because self-duality avoids the extra choice between a single complex channel, a paired `((chi, bar chi))` scalar-amplitude route, or a matrix fallback.
- In that conditional regime, tau is the cleanest exact-`S`-slot candidate presently on the table, because the quotient-phase object is scalar and self-dual from the start.

Missing:

- The source theorem itself.
- Background identification and convergence / regularization.
- Proof that the family theorem returns the exact background-subtracted scalar used by the manuscript's local deformation.
- The five tau localization checkpoints: source decomposition and curvature/tail package; denominator comparability and holomorphy radius; same-point / mixed-block scaling hierarchy; preserved whitening and boundary estimate; growth bookkeeping.
- The remainder-dominance step and independent odd/transverse package still needed after exact scalar identification.

So self-duality buys a cleaner channel choice and removes some phase baggage, but it does not by itself close the exact-slot problem. The hard work remains concentrated in source realization and local transport.

Strongest objection

`materially cleaner` is easy to overstate. From current scope alone, self-duality cleans up only the front end of the route: phase selection and scalar-channel choice. The dominant unresolved work is still the tau-specific source realization and local control package. So if one measures difficulty by the remaining missing lemmas rather than by the choice of candidate object, the path is not yet materially cleaner in a closure-relevant sense.

Needed for promotion

- A tau quotient/source theorem that produces the exact background-subtracted scalar occupying the manuscript's `S(m)` slot.
- A proof that this scalar enters the existing corrected local deformation with the required positivity and normalization.
- Closure of the five localization checkpoints from `tau_localization.md`.
- The post-identification remainder-dominance and odd/transverse packages.
- An adversarial check that no hidden family-specific background term survives outside the claimed scalar slot.

Honest verdict: tau is the cleanest candidate if the project wants one exact positive `S`-slot target beyond zeta, but only conditionally on a source theorem that has not been built. Self-duality removes the Dirichlet phase ambiguity; it does not remove the main realization gap.
