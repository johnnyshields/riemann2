# Claim

Pairing by \
`Xi_\chi(s)=\Lambda(s,\chi)\Lambda(s,\bar\chi)` and the strip-edge quotient \
`\Phi_\chi^{pair}(s)=Xi_\chi(2s-1)/Xi_\chi(2s)` removes the residual root-number phase at the front end, but it does not by itself identify the manuscript's exact local `S(m)` slot or the full background term. The smallest remaining safe theorem burden for the paired route is an exact paired source theorem on singularity-free compact intervals that carries full bookkeeping and exact local-slot identification. That burden is smaller than a full realization program, but larger than root-number cancellation or gamma/trivial-zero/pole tidying alone.
