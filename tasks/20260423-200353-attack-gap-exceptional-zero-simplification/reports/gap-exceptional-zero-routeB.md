# Claim

Avoiding exceptional-zero complications is not enough, from current scope alone, to justify `primitive nonprincipal complex` as the best first paired-source target in any strong theorem-burden sense. The safe conclusion is narrower: for the paired theorem-facing formulation, primitive nonprincipal complex characters remain the most natural first subclass because pairing does real structural work there and because one avoids real-character front-end complications, including the exceptional-zero corridor. But that gain is only a boundary simplification. The dominant burden still lies in exact source-to-slot identification, background unification, convergence / regularization, denominator comparability, corrected whitening, and odd/transverse realization. So the exceptional-zero point should carry secondary, not decisive, weight in the priority note.
