# Claim

The paired-slot theorem boundary is narrower than `full local transport` but wider than `source theorem alone`.

To make the exact coefficient identity meaningful in manuscript form,
`\Delta_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m,\sigma)=S_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m)A_{\mathrm{val},\chi}^{\mathrm{pair}}(m,\sigma)+R_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m,\sigma)`,
the theorem hypotheses must already build in the minimal local analytic setup that defines each term on the left and identifies the first-order value direction on the right.

Minimal hypothesis boundary for meaning:

1. an exact paired source package on compact intervals with unified paired background bookkeeping `B_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}`, so that `S_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m)=q_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m)-B_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m)` is an exact scalar and not a surrogate;
2. the same local symmetric normal form used by the manuscript, so that a paired local deformation `\Delta_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(m,\sigma)` is defined in the same channel;
3. enough corrected-whitening admissibility to make that local deformation well-posed: holomorphic same-point and mixed blocks on a microscopic disk, positive same-point blocks, and hence a holomorphic inverse-square-root whitening map;
4. a first-order paired value-channel derivative `A_{\mathrm{val},\chi}^{\mathrm{pair}}` in that corrected local object.

These are `inside` the paired-slot theorem boundary because without them the phrase `exact coefficient of the value-channel derivative` is not yet a precise identity.

Genuinely downstream hypotheses:

1. any smallness statement on `R_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}`, especially `\Psi(R_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}})=o((x/B)S_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}})`;
2. the `S_2=o(xQ^2S)` remainder-dominance condition or variable-`x` calibration uniformity;
3. the corrected odd/transverse realization package: realized odd scalar, post-`\Phi_K` transport, and microscopic boundary bounds.

Honest boundary sentence:

`The paired-slot theorem must already assume or prove the local analytic setup needed to define the corrected paired deformation and its first-order value direction exactly; remainder dominance and odd/transverse control start only after that exact identity is in place.`

# Evidence

Proved from the manuscript and supplied notes:

- The notes already separate `exact scalar-slot identification` from both remainder dominance and the odd/transverse package, so those latter burdens are downstream rather than part of the slot theorem endpoint.
- In the manuscript, the coefficient identity is not stated in raw source variables. It is stated inside the corrected whitened local object: `\Delta_\zeta=\widehat\Omega_\zeta^{\corr,R}-\widehat\Omega_{\bg}^{\corr,R}=S(m)A_{\val}+R_\zeta`. So the exact slot is meaningful only after the corrected local deformation has been defined.
- The proof of that decomposition explicitly uses the analytic whitening map `\mathcal W(G_-,N,G_+)=G_-^{-1/2}NG_+^{-1/2}` and the fact that it is holomorphic where the same-point blocks stay uniformly positive definite. This shows that some corrected-whitening admissibility is part of the coefficient identity itself, not merely later odd-package machinery.
- The manuscript and transport notes isolate `A_{\val}` transport as only a first-order local statement, conditional on the same local symmetric normal form and a family-level corrected whitening/decomposition theorem. That places the existence of a paired `A_{\mathrm{val},\chi}^{\mathrm{pair}}` on the `meaningful identity` side of the boundary.
- The denominator note narrows denominator comparability to a subtheorem supporting omitted-smooth holomorphy and corrected-whitening admissibility. So its role here is only front-end support for defining the corrected local object, not a standalone downstream theorem.
- By contrast, the manuscript's calibration theorem and the remainder-dominance note only begin after one already has `\Delta=S A_{\val}+R`; the extra hypothesis is then `\Psi(R)=o((x/B)S)` or sufficient forms such as `S_2=o(xQ^2S)`.
- Likewise, the odd-package note says the universal odd projector starts only once the family already provides a realized odd scalar with microscopic boundary control. That is a separate theorem target after exact slot identification.

Conditional on a paired-family transport analogue:

- The paired-slot theorem can likely keep only the `admissibility fragment` of corrected whitening inside its hypotheses: holomorphy on the microscopic disk, positive same-point blocks, and the symmetric normal form needed to define `A_{\mathrm{val},\chi}^{\mathrm{pair}}`.
- Full quantitative corrected-whitening transport, preserved `Q^{-3}` gain, and post-`\Phi_K` transfer estimates appear not to be needed merely to state the coefficient identity. They become relevant once one estimates `R_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}` or builds the transverse scalar.
- Denominator comparability therefore seems to enter the slot theorem only indirectly, to the extent needed to secure omitted-smooth holomorphy and whitening admissibility. It does not need to appear as a theorem endpoint of its own inside the paired-slot statement.

Missing:

- A paired-family theorem that explicitly states the minimal analytic hypotheses under which `\Delta_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}`, `A_{\mathrm{val},\chi}^{\mathrm{pair}}`, and `R_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}` are all well-defined in exact manuscript form.
- A proof that the minimal corrected-whitening admissibility assumptions can be separated cleanly from the stronger quantitative transport estimates used later for remainder bounds and odd-package boundary control.
- A paired-family construction showing that the unified background `B_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}` plus the local symmetric normal form indeed identify the exact manuscript slot, rather than only a nearby positive strip-edge kernel.

Honest verdict: the exact coefficient identity already forces a nontrivial local analytic setup into the paired-slot theorem hypotheses. The minimal imported package is `source + symmetric local normal form + corrected-whitening admissibility + first-order A_val direction`. Remainder dominance and odd/transverse work are genuinely downstream once that identity exists.

# Strongest objection

The weakest point in this boundary is whether `corrected-whitening admissibility` really can be split cleanly into a minimal hypothesis fragment for meaning and a later quantitative fragment for estimates. The notes say corrected whitening is separate from slot realization, but the manuscript proof of the coefficient identity itself passes through holomorphic whitening. So the paired theorem may be forced to import more of the corrected-whitening package than current high-level notes admit.
