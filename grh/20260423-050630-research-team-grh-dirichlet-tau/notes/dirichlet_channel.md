Date: 2026-04-23
Focused attack: `tasks/20260423-053136-attack-gap-dirichlet-channel/`

## Main conclusion

From current local scope, the honest Dirichlet statement is only a
candidate-selection statement.

- The current RH draft does not realize a primitive-Dirichlet analogue of the
  corrected local block, odd transverse scalar, calibration interface, or final
  contradiction.
- The paired `\((\chi,\bar\chi)\)` scalar route is the more conservative first
  scalar target, not a proved safer route.
- The rotated single completed channel is no longer just a bare reality
  candidate: after the quotient-phase attack it is best read as a conditional
  phase-channel candidate.
- A matrix-valued package is a fallback if both scalar routes fail.

After the scattering-generalization attack, the sharper target is not merely a
real scalar candidate. It is a background-subtracted completed-object scalar
analogous to `S(m)`: real, nonnegative, and structurally positioned as the
coefficient of the leading local value channel.

## Why the draft is stricter than a simple reality argument

The paper needs more than a real critical-line quantity.

It uses:

- a real scalar amplitude replacing `S(m)`;
- sign-compatibility strong enough to compare with `u^2`;
- a one-dimensional leading-channel decomposition;
- corrected whitening and oddness in the realized channel;
- microscopic denominator comparability and boundary control.

Critical-line reality alone does not supply that package.

The newer scattering note sharpens this further: the manuscript's zeta scalar
is not just real, but a positive strip-edge zero contribution. So the real
Dirichlet problem is to realize an analogue of that positive scalar in a
completed Dirichlet package.

The later single-channel attack sharpens the comparison once more: conditional
on a single-channel quotient source theorem, the single completed quotient also
has conditional upstairs strip-edge kernel positivity. But from current scope
alone, that still does not identify the manuscript's exact `S(m)` slot.

## Safest current ranking

1. Paired scalar `\((\chi,\bar\chi)\)` route: more conservative first scalar
   target because reality and nonnegativity are built in at the scalar level,
   and it looks likelier to support a positive `S`-analogue from current scope
   alone.
2. Rotated single completed channel: conditional only; currently supported only
   as a phase-channel candidate, not as a full calibration/oddness channel.
3. Matrix-valued package: fallback if scalar routes fail to reproduce the local
   oddness and calibration machinery.

## What is still missing

1. A completed-`L` local realization theorem in one chosen primitive-Dirichlet
   channel.
2. A real sign-compatible scalar amplitude that actually plays the role of
   `S(m)`.
3. Reproved corrected whitening, denominator comparability, and boundary
   estimates in that channel.
4. Proof that the oddness mechanism and scalar functional survive, or a
   replacement that does.

## Honest boundary

The portable part remains only Layer 1 from `notes/local_package_theorem.md`:
the abstract odd-germ / odd-projector mechanism. Dirichlet realization of the
calibration layer and any contradiction theorem remains open.

The scattering-generalization attack improves channel selection only. It does
not change that open boundary.

The quotient-phase attack sharpens that sentence further: the single completed
quotient is a serious phase candidate, but still only at the phase front end.
The paired route remains the conservative scalar-amplitude target.

The single-channel attack makes the qualifier precise: the paired route remains
the conservative scalar-amplitude target `from current scope alone`.
