# Quotient Phase Note

Date: 2026-04-23
Focused attack: `tasks/20260423-133143-attack-gap-quotient-phase-generalization/`

## Main conclusion

The completed strip-edge quotient

`phi_F(s) = Lambda_F(2s-1) / Lambda_F(2s)`

is a serious boundary phase candidate for primitive Dirichlet and tau, but only
at the phase-channel level.

Under the standard completed-`L` functional equation and conjugation symmetry,
the quotient is naturally unimodular on `Re(s)=1/2`.

## What this upgrades

- Primitive Dirichlet single-channel route: from `reality candidate` to
  `conditional phase-channel candidate`.
- Ramanujan `\tau`: from a general self-dual candidate object to the cleanest
  phase-channel candidate in the current notes.

## What this does not upgrade

It does not supply:

- a realized positive `S(m)`-analogue;
- the leading value-channel coefficient theorem;
- denominator comparability;
- corrected whitening;
- microscopic boundary control;
- any localization or contradiction theorem.

So the right wording is: `phase-channel upgrade only`.

## Dirichlet stance after this attack

- Single-channel quotient: conditional phase candidate.
- Paired `((chi, bar chi))` route: still the more conservative scalar-amplitude
  target.
- Matrix package: still fallback if scalar-amplitude realization fails.

## Tau stance after this attack

- Self-dual quotient: cleanest phase candidate.
- None of the five localization checkpoints from `tau_localization.md` is closed
  by unimodularity alone.
