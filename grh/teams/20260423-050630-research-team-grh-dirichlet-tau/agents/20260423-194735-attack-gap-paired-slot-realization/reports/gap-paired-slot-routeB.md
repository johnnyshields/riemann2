Claim

Exact paired slot realization is a meaningful source theorem, but even if it identifies the exact background-subtracted scalar occupying the manuscript's paired `S(m)` slot, it does not by itself close the downstream portability chain. What follows automatically is narrow; the main remaining burdens are independent and theorem-sized.

Status

open

Evidence

Proved:
- From the supplied notes, if a family theorem yields the exact scalar in the manuscript's `S(m)` slot, then the manuscript's existing local algebra already supplies the leading-channel coefficient theorem. No second coefficient theorem of the form `Delta = S A_val + R` is needed.
- In the paired quotient packaging, once an exact paired-source theorem with exact local-slot identification is available, the one-zero strip-edge kernel and its positivity are automatic upstairs.

Conditional on exact paired slot realization:
- The calibrated value-channel subchain reduces to the remainder-dominance task `Psi_{x,d}(R) = o((x/B) S)` or, in the current sufficient form, `S_2 = o(x Q^2 S)`.
- The front-end scalar-amplitude identification is then no longer the active obstruction for that value-channel subchain.

Missing:
- Remainder dominance is still needed; exact slot realization does not imply it.
- Denominator comparability, corrected whitening control, and compact-interval background identification remain family-specific analytic burdens.
- The odd/transverse package remains independent: existence of a corrected real odd transverse scalar, its symmetry mechanism after correction/whitening, and the boundary estimate on `|s| = rho_0 / Q` do not follow from slot realization alone.
- Boundary control is still separate even in the paired route.
- Variable-`x` calibration uniformity is not solved from this scope alone.

So the safe consequence chain is:
1. exact paired source theorem with exact local-slot identification;
2. remainder-dominance theorem for the calibrated value channel;
3. separate corrected odd/transverse realization-and-boundary theorem;
4. additional family-specific denominator/whitening/uniformity control where required.

Strongest objection

This analysis is only as strong as the premise that the family theorem delivers the exact manuscript slot and not a nearby positive scalar with similar sign behavior. If the realized paired object misses the exact local slot, then even the claimed automatic coefficient consequences do not start, and the burden reverts to source-to-slot identification rather than downstream calibration.
