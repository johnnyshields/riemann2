- Claim

  The paired `((chi, bar chi))` route remains the conservative scalar-amplitude target. The completed single quotient does not overtake it after the quotient-phase and universal-kernel attacks; it narrows the gap by upgrading the single-channel route from a bare reality candidate to a conditional phase-channel candidate only.

- Evidence

  Proved:
  The current notes explicitly separate the upgrades. `notes/quotient_phase.md` states that `phi_F(s)=Lambda_F(2s-1)/Lambda_F(2s)` is a serious boundary phase candidate on `Re(s)=1/2`, but that this does not supply a positive `S(m)`-analogue, the leading value-channel coefficient theorem, denominator comparability, corrected whitening, microscopic boundary control, or any localization/contradiction theorem; it therefore labels the change a `phase-channel upgrade only` and keeps the paired `((chi, bar chi))` route as the more conservative scalar-amplitude target (lines 8-18, 25-43). `notes/universal_source_kernel.md` sharpens the same separation: conditional on a family-specific quotient/source theorem, the single-zero quotient-log-derivative kernel and its positivity are universal upstairs, but the source theorem, background identification, convergence, and placement into the manuscript's exact `S(m)` slot remain family-specific (lines 8-35, 39-44). `notes/positive_s_implication.md` closes one logical branch but only after exact scalar identification: if a family theorem yields the exact scalar occupying the manuscript's `S(m)` slot, then the existing local algebra already provides the leading-channel coefficient theorem; quotient/phase data alone does not produce that scalar, and remainder dominance, odd/transverse structure, and family-specific denominator/whitening/boundary control still remain open (lines 8-16, 17-38, 42-50). `notes/dirichlet_channel.md` already drew the same ranking before this focused check: the paired route is the more conservative first scalar target, while the rotated single completed channel is supported only as a conditional phase-channel candidate (lines 14-19, 45-52, 75-77).

  The paper's zeta architecture matches that separation. The argument uses a background-subtracted value scalar `S(m)=q_zeta(m)-B_zeta(m)` as the coefficient of the leading value-channel deformation (paper `proof_of_rh.tex` lines 285-292, 426-486, 739-779, 1497-1566). The corrected finite-`s` package then separately requires denominator comparability and holomorphic extension for the omitted-smooth part (lines 856-903), corrected finite-`s` holomorphic whitening (lines 1392-1458), and an odd transverse scalar `H_m(s)=Phi_K(\widehat\Omega^\corr_zeta(s;m))` with boundary estimate and odd microscopic expansion (lines 2214-2322). The paper also proves that the leading value channel is annihilated by `Phi_K`, so the odd transverse package is structurally distinct from the scalar-amplitude package (lines 567-599, 2218-2221, 2253-2265). This is the exact distinction the mission asked for: positivity of the scalar is not the same task as oddness, whitening, or boundary control.

  Conditional on a family-specific source theorem yielding the exact Dirichlet scalar in the manuscript's `S(m)` slot, the positive-`S` note says the leading-channel coefficient theorem would already be inherited from the manuscript's local algebra. That narrows the paired-route gap substantially. But the same note leaves open the remainder-dominance step, the independent odd/transverse package, and the family-specific denominator/whitening/boundary estimates, so the quotient completion still does not by itself realize the scalar-amplitude package.

  Missing:
  No current note gives a primitive-Dirichlet theorem that the completed single quotient itself produces the exact local value scalar occupying the manuscript's `S(m)` slot. No current note shows that the single quotient carries the corrected whitening package, the odd transverse cancellation channel, or the microscopic boundary estimate in the Dirichlet setting. Therefore the single completed quotient has not overtaken the paired route as the conservative scalar-amplitude target.

- Dependencies

  The ranking depends on reading the current draft architecture conservatively: scalar-amplitude realization, odd/transverse annihilation, corrected whitening, and boundary control are separate checkpoints unless a theorem explicitly links them. It also depends on accepting the scoped conditional from `positive_s_implication.md`: exact identification of the family scalar with the manuscript's `S(m)` slot would import the leading-coefficient theorem, but not the remaining transverse and boundary packages.

- Strongest objection

  The strongest objection is that the universal-kernel and positive-`S` notes together may have reduced the scalar-amplitude gap for the single completed quotient more than this ranking allows. If a primitive-Dirichlet quotient/source theorem were close enough to identify the exact `S(m)` slot scalar, then the paired route's main advertised advantage could collapse quickly, because the coefficient theorem would already come for free from the existing local algebra. In that scenario the single route might become equally conservative or better. But that theorem is not present in the current material, and from current scope alone the quotient result remains phase-front-end only.

- Needed for promotion

  3. An independent Dirichlet realization of the corrected odd/transverse package: the analogue of `Phi_K(A_{\val})=0`, odd microscopic expansion, and the `N`-point cancellation channel.
  4. Family-specific denominator comparability, corrected whitening, convergence/regularization, and microscopic boundary control in the completed Dirichlet setting.
  5. Only after items 1-4 would there be evidence that the single completed quotient overtakes, rather than merely narrows, the paired `((chi, bar chi))` route.
