Safe:

- as a theorem-facing `2-step blueprint`: `(1)` compact-interval paired source-package blueprint, then `(2)` exact paired local `S_chi^pair(m)`-slot identification with source-normalized scalar compatibility built into that second step;

Not safe:

- or as saying the next paired burden is already known to be slot identification ahead of background/convergence/multiplicity work without the theorem-facing qualifier.

Honest verdict on the questions:

- `What is the strongest safe roadmap sentence?` `From the current notes alone, the safe paired roadmap is a theorem-facing 2-step blueprint: first a compact-interval paired source-package blueprint, then a second theorem burden proving together exact local S_chi^pair(m)-slot identification and source-normalized scalar compatibility; unified background, convergence/regularization, multiplicity, and whitening-canonicity remain explicit missing burdens rather than settled earlier steps.`

- Proved: the safe theorem-facing shape is a paired `2-step` blueprint; the compact package must stop before exact slot realization and local whitening; the value-channel formulation is only a `conditional, source-normalized interface`.
- Conditional: after the compact package is fixed, the second burden can be packaged as exact slot identification plus source-normalized scalar match.
