Claim

The six portability reports are mostly grounded correctly in `paper/proof_of_rh.tex`, but several claims mix three levels that must stay separate: what the draft already proves for the zeta-side architecture, what is only suggested by the draft as a portability template, and what is standard external completed-`L`-function input not sourced locally. The strongest manuscript-supported port-back edits are editorial abstraction-boundary edits, not theorem-level portability claims. No report appears to invent a nonexistent manuscript statement, but a few exact refs are too coarse or omit that the key support is external rather than in-repo.

Strongest objection

The main risk is not fabricated citation. It is category drift: the reports often move smoothly from “the zeta draft is written in a generic-looking local language” to “therefore the same mechanism should port to completed `L`-functions.” From manuscript scope alone, that second step is not proved. The draft itself repeatedly says the late contradiction needs RH-specific structure, and even earlier calibration and boundary estimates use zeta-side analytic packaging. So a future synthesis that does not mark the external completed-`L` facts and the missing derivations would overstate what is currently grounded in the repo.
