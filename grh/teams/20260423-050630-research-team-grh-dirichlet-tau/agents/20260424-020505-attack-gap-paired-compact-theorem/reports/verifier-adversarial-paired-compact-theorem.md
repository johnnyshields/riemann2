# verifier-adversarial-paired-compact-theorem

- **Claim**

  From current scope alone, the compact-interval formulation looks like the right first theorem-facing shape only in a scoped blueprint sense: it is the right local scaffold for a paired source package, not yet a safe standalone paired theorem conclusion. The safest shape is still a two-step local program:
  \
  1. a family-specific compact-interval paired source theorem for \(\Phi_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(s)=\Xi_\chi(2s-1)/\Xi_\chi(2s)\);
  2. a separate exact local patch-to-slot identification theorem showing that the background-subtracted localized scalar is exactly the manuscript's paired \(S(m)\) coefficient.

  The strongest safe roadmap sentence now is:

  `from current scope alone, compact-interval localization is the right theorem-facing scaffold for a paired local source package, but the main open burden is still exact paired S(m)-slot realization together with unified paired background, convergence, and multiplicity bookkeeping.`

  Claims that need scoped weakening now are any claims that compact localization:

  - already gives the paired theorem;
  - makes the exact paired \(S(m)\) slot automatic;
  - reduces the burden to local bookkeeping only;
  - transfers the zeta source package cleanly;
  - leaves only background identification.

- **Status**

  open

- **Evidence**

  **proved**

  The note set consistently supports compact intervals as a theorem-facing local shape, but not as a closed theorem conclusion. The source-package transfer note says the zeta package is only a blueprint and that what transfers formally is limited to the quotient shell, the upstairs one-zero kernel shape, and the idea of a localized compact-interval source theorem; it also states that exact paired \(S(m)\)-slot realization, unified paired background identification, paired compact-interval convergence/regularization, and theorem-facing multiplicity bookkeeping do not transfer formally (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/source_package_transfer.md:8-15`, `:17-39`). The paired-source note says the clean theorem-facing object is the strip-edge quotient \(\Phi_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}\), but pairing does not remove the need for exact source-to-slot background identification on compact intervals and does not by itself fix the theorem-sized source gap or local-slot identification (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/dirichlet_paired_source.md:20-27`, `:49-56`, `:66-87`). The paired-slot note already splits the burden into upstairs paired source realization and downstairs exact local-slot identification, which is the same two-step structure the compact-interval formulation still needs (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/paired_slot_realization.md:8-24`). The first-milestone note supports this as the first non-zeta exact-source milestone, while explicitly warning that it must not swallow downstream work (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/first_milestone.md:11-18`, `:29-36`).

  **conditional on [a family-specific paired compact-interval source theorem]**

  Under a genuine compact-interval source theorem for the paired quotient, compact localization is the right first theorem-facing shape because it gives the conservative local place to state quotient normalization, one-zero contribution, regularization, background subtraction, and multiplicity conventions without smuggling in boundary control or endgame consequences. In that scoped sense, route A is right that the theorem should be local rather than global first, and route B is right that the gain is theorem shape rather than theorem closure (`tasks/20260424-020505-attack-gap-paired-compact-theorem/reports/gap-paired-compact-routeA.md:5-15`, `:23-32`; `tasks/20260424-020505-attack-gap-paired-compact-theorem/reports/gap-paired-compact-routeB.md:3-10`, `:25-30`).

  **missing**

  No supplied note proves the compact-interval theorem itself, and no supplied note collapses the patch-to-slot step into the source theorem. The missing items are still theorem-sized: a family-specific compact-interval convergence/regularization statement, a unified theorem-facing paired background term, exact proof that the localized background-subtracted scalar is the manuscript's paired \(S(m)\) slot rather than a nearby positive strip-edge scalar, and explicit multiplicity bookkeeping (`grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/source_package_transfer.md:33-39`; `grh/20260423-050630-research-team-grh-dirichlet-tau/notes/dirichlet_paired_source.md:66-73`; `tasks/20260424-020505-attack-gap-paired-compact-theorem/reports/gap-paired-compact-routeA.md:29-32`; `tasks/20260424-020505-attack-gap-paired-compact-theorem/reports/gap-paired-compact-routeB.md:31-39`).

  The theorem-facing sentence therefore needs narrower scope than either `the compact-interval paired theorem is the right first theorem` or `the zeta patch structure transfers cleanly`. The safe sentence is about scaffold only. Anything stronger risks blurring local source packaging with exact slot realization.

  Honest verdict: the compact-interval formulation is the right first theorem-facing shape only as a localized paired source-package blueprint. From current scope alone, it is not yet a safe theorem conclusion, because exact paired \(S(m)\)-slot realization and unified background/convergence/multiplicity bookkeeping remain separate missing burdens.

- **Dependencies**

  - a family-specific compact-interval source theorem for `\Phi_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(s)=\Xi_\chi(2s-1)/\Xi_\chi(2s)`;
  - an exact theorem-facing definition of the paired localized background term, including conductor/scaling, gamma-derivative, and any trivial-zero or pole corrections;

  - explicit multiplicity and regularization conventions with no hidden correction term;

- **Strongest objection**

  The strongest objection to this adversarial verdict is that a sufficiently sharp compact-interval paired theorem might force the patch-to-slot identification and unified background package at the same time, so that the two-step split is overly cautious. Current scope does not prove that collapse. From current scope alone, treating source packaging and exact slot realization as already fused would be overclaim.

- **Needed for promotion**

  - state the compact-interval paired theorem with exact local variables, hypotheses, regularization, and outputs;
  - prove interval-by-interval convergence/regularization for the paired quotient in theorem-facing form;
  - prove that the localized background subtraction is exactly the manuscript's paired `S(m)` slot, not merely a nearby positive local scalar;
  - make the multiplicity bookkeeping explicit enough that no factor-of-`2`, branch, or trivial-zero correction is deferred to prose;
  - keep all theorem-facing language scoped to `localized paired source-plus-slot blueprint` until the previous items are closed.
