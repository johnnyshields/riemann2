## Claim

Compact-interval localization is a real gain for the paired theorem, but it is a gain in theorem shape, not theorem closure. From current scope alone, it supports a localized paired source theorem stated interval-by-interval and it makes a zeta-like patch decomposition plausible. It does not collapse the paired burden to local bookkeeping, because the exact paired `S(m)` slot and the unified paired background `B_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}` are still theorem-sized family-specific inputs.

So the paired theorem should mirror the zeta 2-step patch structure only in the following scoped sense:

1. a compact-interval paired source package;
2. a separate exact patch-to-slot identification theorem that shows the localized background-subtracted scalar is exactly the manuscript's paired local value-channel coefficient.

Without step 2, compact localization produces a local source object, not yet the manuscript slot.

## Evidence

Proved.

- The supplied notes already isolate compact-interval localization as one of the zeta package's theorem-level components, not as cosmetic presentation. The formal blueprint pieces are quotient normalization, single-zero contribution, compact-interval summation / regularization, background identification, and multiplicity convention.
- The same notes say the paired transfer is formal only upstairs. In particular, they explicitly list as formal only the quotient shell, the upstairs one-zero kernel shape, and the idea of a localized compact-interval source theorem.
- The paired-source and paired-slot notes both keep exact local-slot realization separate from the source package. They repeatedly say the load-bearing target is not just a paired source theorem, but `source-plus-slot realization`, with two linked burdens: upstairs paired source realization and downstairs exact local-slot identification.
- The paired-background note sharpens this further: pairing removes the residual root-number/front-end asymmetry, but not the need for exact source-to-slot background identification on compact intervals. So compact localization does not eliminate slot-specific bookkeeping; it only localizes where that bookkeeping must be done.

Conditional on [a family-specific compact-interval paired source theorem for `\Phi_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}(s)=\Xi_\chi(2s-1)/\Xi_\chi(2s)`].

- The paired theorem should mirror the zeta 2-step patch structure as a blueprint. The natural paired analogue is: first prove a compact-interval regularized source formula for the paired quotient; then patch that local formula to the manuscript coefficient by identifying the exact background-subtracted scalar in each interval with the paired `S(m)` slot.
- Under that first step, one gains a controlled local theorem format: interval-by-interval summation / regularization, a localized single-zero contribution, and a local place to state multiplicity and background conventions. This is the main gain from compact localization.
- If the second step is also proved, the existing manuscript local algebra can then attach the upstairs positive kernel to the actual paired value-channel coefficient. In that scoped sense, the compact theorem would not merely imitate zeta structurally; it would supply the missing local interface.

Missing.

- No supplied note proves a paired compact-interval convergence / regularization theorem. This is listed as one of the non-formal pieces that does not transfer automatically from zeta.
- No supplied note proves that the localized paired background can be unified into an exact theorem-facing `B_\chi^{\mathrm{pair}}` including conductor/scaling, gamma-derivative, and trivial-zero or pole corrections with no leftover local term.
- No supplied note proves exact patch-to-slot identification: that the localized background-subtracted scalar on a compact interval is exactly the manuscript's paired `S(m)` coefficient, rather than a nearby positive strip-edge scalar.
- No supplied note shows that multiplicity bookkeeping becomes automatic in the paired compact theorem. It remains part of the theorem-facing package.
- Even after a compact paired source-plus-slot theorem, the downstream burdens remain separate: remainder dominance, corrected odd/transverse realization, denominator comparability, corrected whitening, boundary control, and any calibration uniformity.

This is why the safe answer is: compact localization helps the paired theorem look like the zeta patch architecture, but from current scope alone it does not remove the global or slot-specific theorem burden. It relocates that burden into an interval-by-interval package that still has to be proved.

Honest verdict: compact intervals buy a cleaner local theorem format and make a 2-step paired patch strategy the right blueprint. They do not by themselves prove the paired theorem or make the exact paired `S(m)` slot automatic. The main missing item is still a family-specific local source-to-slot identification theorem with unified paired background bookkeeping.

The strongest objection is that this may still understate what compact localization buys. A sufficiently sharp compact paired formula might force the background subtraction and local-slot identification at the same time, so that the second patch step is not genuinely independent. Current notes do not prove that collapse. From current scope alone, the safe reading is that compact localization gives the right local theorem scaffold, while exact paired slot realization remains a separate missing theorem burden.
