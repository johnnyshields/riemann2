## Claim

From current scope alone, the right `first non-zeta milestone` is the
primitive-Dirichlet paired source-plus-slot realization, not the
corrected-whitening transport bundle, but only after narrowing the label.

The safe distinction is:

- `first non-zeta exact-source realization milestone` = paired
  source-plus-slot realization;
- `earliest partial family-level transport bundle` = corrected-whitening
  transport.

So the strongest safe milestone wording now is:

`From current scope alone, the first non-zeta exact-source realization
milestone is the primitive Dirichlet paired source theorem together with exact
local-slot identification; corrected-whitening transport is a separate earlier
partial bundle, not a substitute for exact source realization.`

open

## Evidence

**Proved / established in current scope.**

- The priority note already fixes the top non-zeta target under an explicit
  criterion: `first non-zeta realization` points to the primitive Dirichlet
  paired-scalar exact-source theorem, while corrected-whitening is discussed
  elsewhere only as an intermediate bundle. That directly supports the paired
  route as the right answer once the criterion is stated. 
- The paired-slot note sharpens the theorem target to the exact paired local
  value-channel coefficient and explicitly says this is the cleanest current
  `first non-zeta exact-source` target, with downstream remainder,
  odd/transverse, denominator/whitening, boundary, and calibration burdens left
  separate.
- The Dirichlet paired-source note strengthens that conclusion: the clean
  theorem-facing object is `Phi_chi^pair(s)=Xi_chi(2s-1)/Xi_chi(2s)` with
  `Xi_chi(s)=Lambda(s,chi)Lambda(s,bar chi)`, and the strongest safe theorem
  candidate is still `exact paired-scalar source theorem with full bookkeeping
  and exact local-slot identification`.
- The corrected-whitening note is narrower by design. It isolates only three
  pieces: denominator comparability plus omitted-smooth holomorphy,
  holomorphic corrected whitening with positive same-point blocks, and
  preserved post-`Phi_K` transfer scale. The note itself says this is a
  `partial intermediate bundle`, not the whole family package.
- The `A_val` transport and boundary-package notes both confirm that
  corrected-whitening does not realize the family-level `Delta = S A_val + R`
  theorem, does not identify the exact manuscript-style scalar slot, and does
  not deliver the later odd/transverse or boundary package. That blocks using
  corrected-whitening as the first non-zeta milestone if `milestone` is meant
  to name the first realized non-zeta source mechanism rather than merely an
  earlier transport lemma package.

**Conditional on the milestone wording being narrowed.**

- If `milestone` is narrowed to `first non-zeta exact-source realization
  milestone`, then the paired source-plus-slot theorem is the right first
  milestone from current scope alone.
- If `milestone` instead means `smallest honest intermediate theorem package`,
  then corrected-whitening transport is the smaller object, but that is a
  different criterion and should not replace the exact-source milestone.
- Conditional on proving the paired source-plus-slot theorem, the existing
  local coefficient algebra becomes usable for the calibrated value-channel
  subchain, and then remainder dominance is the next bottleneck only for that
  subchain, not for the whole portability program.

**Missing.**

- A proof that the two paired burdens really collapse into one proof interface
  rather than remaining two linked theorem burdens: upstairs exact paired source
  realization and downstairs exact local-slot identification.
- A proof of the exact paired background bookkeeping on compact intervals.
- A proof of exact local-slot identification downstairs.
- Any theorem establishing remainder dominance, corrected odd/transverse
  realization, corrected transverse realization-and-boundary, or variable-`x`
  calibration uniformity.
- Any basis for saying corrected-whitening alone yields an exact non-zeta
  manuscript-style `S(m)` analogue.

## Strongest objection

The strongest objection is that the paired source-plus-slot statement still
bundles two serious theorem burdens under one milestone label, while
corrected-whitening has a cleaner and smaller proof boundary. That objection is
real and it blocks any claim that paired source-plus-slot is the `smallest`
honest milestone. But it does not overturn the milestone choice once the label
is made precise: corrected-whitening still does not realize the exact paired
source mechanism or the manuscript-style local `S(m)` slot, so from current
scope alone it is too weak to replace the first non-zeta exact-source
milestone. The safe fix is not to demote paired source-plus-slot below
corrected-whitening, but to weaken the wording so that the criterion is stated
and the two linked burdens are acknowledged.
