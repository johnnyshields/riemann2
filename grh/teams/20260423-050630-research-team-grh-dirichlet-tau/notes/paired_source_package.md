# Paired Source Package Note

Date: 2026-04-24

## Main conclusion

The compact paired source theorem candidate is safe only as a compact-interval
source-package blueprint.

The later summation attack sharpens the internal bottleneck:

- the first real theorem burden inside that blueprint is paired compact-interval
  convergence / regularization;
- together with unified `B_chi^pair` and multiplicity bookkeeping.

The later regularization attack sharpens the safe wording one step further:

- the localized theorem shape itself is still only a blueprint target;
- the first genuinely new paired burden is exactly compact-interval
  convergence/regularization, with unified background and multiplicity still
  coupled to it.

The later regularization-plan verifier tightens the endpoint:

- compact-interval regularization is the first real missing burden inside the
  blueprint;
- but the safe promoted endpoint remains the full compact source-package
  blueprint/schema, not a standalone regularization theorem.

The later regularization-package verifier sharpens the safe wording again:

- any theorem-like compact decomposition language must stay explicitly
  schematic/blueprint-level;
- claims about `B_chi^pair`, multiplicity, exact `S(m)` matching, or
  canonicality still need scoped weakening at this stage.

The later multiplicity attack fixes the safest theorem-facing placement:

- keep multiplicity out of the paired theorem body for now;
- but do not hide it as a loose global convention either;
- safest placement is immediately after the theorem as explicit bookkeeping
  material tied to the same compact summation/regularization package.

The later paired-background-placement attack yields the same verdict for the
unified paired background term:

- keep unified `B_chi^pair` out of the theorem body for now;
- but place it immediately after the theorem as explicit compact-source
  bookkeeping, not as a remote convention.

The later paired-background-bookkeeping attack sharpens the safe content of that
bookkeeping block:

- the safe named pieces are conductor/scaling, gamma-derivative, and
  trivial-zero or pole corrections under one canonical symbol `B_chi^pair`;
- but `B_chi^pair` is still safe only as immediate theorem-adjacent
  bookkeeping, not as canonical theorem-body content.

The later bookkeeping-block attack refines the immediate post-theorem payload:

- it should fix `B_chi^pair`, its safe component list, multiplicity bookkeeping,
  and scoped notation aliases;
- it should stop before exact `S_chi^pair(m)` slot realization, which remains a
  later theorem burden.

It must stop before:

- exact paired `S(m)`-slot realization;
- any local whitening-interface claim.

So the theorem-facing source package is still shaped by the zeta blueprint, but
its first genuinely family-specific burden is the compact summation layer.

Those remain conditional or missing and belong to later theorem steps.

## Safe wording

`the paired source theorem should first be stated as a compact-interval source-package blueprint; exact slot realization and local whitening belong later.`
